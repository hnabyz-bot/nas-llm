1. Purpose
It is intended to be used in management to ensure the quality of products produced by the
company and the safety of hazardous substances, and to provide safe products to the
customers through risk analysis, evaluation and management.
2. Application
This procedure applies to risk management tasks.
3. Definition of Terms
3.1 Benefit
Positive impact/desired outcome on individual’s health, patient care or public health
when using a medical device
3.2 Harm
Physical harm and/or damage to health/property
3.3 Hazard
Potential cause of harm
3.4 Risk
Combination of both possibility and seriousness of harm
3.5 Safety
A state of safety from the unexpected risks of harm
3.6 Risk Analysis
The systematic use of available information to identify hazards and estimate risks
3.7 Safety measure
A device that removes hazards or reduces risks
3.8 Residual Risk
Residual risks after safety control measures

3.9 Risk Assessment
Overall process of risk analysis and evaluation
3.10 Risk Control
A process by which protective actions are implemented and decisions are made to
maintain or reduce risk within a defined range.
3.11 Risk Estimation
Process used to estimate the likelihood of occurrence of a hazard and the severity of it.
3.12 Risk Evaluation
The process of comparing an estimated risk to a given risk tolerance criterion to
determine the acceptability of the risk.
Judgement based on analysis
3.13 Risk Management
Systematic application of management policies, procedures and regulations to the work
of analyzing, evaluating and managing risks
3.14 Risk Management File
A set of records or documents generated by the risk management process

5. Business Procedure
5.1 Risk Management Meeting
5.1.1 The CEO conducts Risk Management Meetings to ensure the suitability and
effectiveness of risk management policies, goals and ISO 14971 standards.
5.1.2 The quality manager must plan and preside over a Risk Management Meeting at
least once a year
5.1.3 Attendees shall be managers designated by the quality manager.
5.1.4 The topics of the Risk Management Meeting are mainly, but not limited to, the
following
1) Review risk management plan
2) Contents and results of risk analysis
3) Measurement of hazards
4) Risk assessment
5) Application of risk control measures
6) Tolerance of risk compared to gain
7) Revision of risk management report
8) Education, training status and qualification certification for risk management
personnel
9) Post-Production risk information
5.1.5 Decisions made at the risk management meeting or instructions from the CEO must
be acted upon and carried out in accordance with the “Corrective and Preventive
Action Ragulations (AQP803)”. The effectiveness of the measures taken must be
confirmed by the quality manager.

5.1.6 Revision dates and results of Risk Management Meetings must be maintained in the
risk management file.
5.2 Risk Management Operation
5.2.1 The CEO must establish a policy on acceptable risks for each product/model.
5.2.2 The quality team must prepare a risk management plan to implement risk
management for the product in compliance with this policy/goal and the
requirements of the ISO 14971 standard and obtain approval from the quality
manager. This plan must include the following
1) Scope of the plan- Product description and life cycle to which the plan applies
2) Verification plan
3) Allocation of responsibility
4) Revision requirements for risk management activities
5) Standards of risk control
6) Environmental risks
7) Hazardous substance risk information
5.2.3 The quality team establishes a risk analysis (steps1-3) according to this plan and
records the results in the risk management file.
1) Description and identification of the product to be analyzed
2) Identification of personnel performing risk analysis
3) Risk analysis date
4) Hazardous substance risk analysis
5.2.4 Risk assessment (step 4)
1) The development team leader determines the identified hazards using the
standards specified in the risk management plan. The risk assessment is
conducted by reviewing and considering the following cases.
(1) Disregarding negligible risks
(2) Individual and combined risk-benefit analysis for all risks
(3) Disqualification of economic considerations

(5) Eliminate risk using all identified risk control methods

2) Hazardous substance risk analysis

5.2.5 Risk Control (steps 5-10)
The quality team leader identifies the following risk control measures, applies them
as priorities, and records them in the risk management file. (step 5)
1) Safe structure by design: Design
2) Protection measures for the product itself or during the process: Packaging
3) Information about safety: User manual, labeling
4) Information about the environment
5.2.6 Verification of risk control measures (step 6)
The quality team leader performs verification of risk control measures and records
it in the risk management file.

2) Risk Assessment
3) Implementation and verification of risk control measures

4) Evaluation of acceptability of residual risk
5.2.1 post-production stage (step 13)
The procedure for obtaining and reviewing information about our products or similar
products is collected by the quality manager in accordance with the vigilance system
of the customer complaint handling regulations (AQP806).
The quality manager must forward this information to the quality team leader to
evaluate the possible appropriateness of the following:
1) Are there any previously unrecognized hazards?
2) Is the assessed risk from the hazard unacceptable?
3) Has the initial assessment been invalidated? If so, the assessment results should be
fed back as input to the risk management process.
If the residual risk or the acceptability of the residual risk changes, the impact on
previously implemented risk control measures should be assessed. The evaluation
results are recorded in the risk management file.

Risk management does not define acceptable risks; it determines acceptable risks.
Risks are categorized into two areas:

1) Widely acceptable Zone (GREEN AREA)
If the risk is low considering the benefits of using the product compared to other
risks, the risk is negligible. In such cases, the risk is acceptable and there is no need
to actively pursue risk control.
2) Unacceptable Zone (RED AREA)
Risks that cannot be reduced can always be judged as unacceptable.
5.4.2 Risk tolerance standards
The level of probability and severity is set to at least 3 levels, and the risk tolerance
standard must be determined according to the allowable risk level for each product

4) Risk Management plan/report Reporter
