General Description

The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are wired or wireless digital flat panel detectors that have been designed for faster, more streamlined approach to digital radiography systems. The detector consists of TFT and Scintillator (CsI) and an electronic device that converts data read through it into digital signals. The detectors support an auto exposure detection technology that allows the detectors to be used without generator integration.

The flat panel sensors of the A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are fabricated using thin film technology based on amorphous silicon technology. Electronically, the sensors are much like conventional photodiode arrays. Each pixel in the array consists of a light‐sensing photodiode and a switching Thin Film Transistor (TFT) in the same electronic circuit. The amorphous silicon photodiode is sensitive to visual light, and its response curve has almost the same performance as human vision. The sensitivity of amorphous silicon photodiodes peaks in green wavelengths, well matched to scintillators such as CsI. It also has excellent linearity according to the exposure dose.

Intended purpose

The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnosis and treatment planning on a real time basis for physicians and radiologists.

Principles of Operation of the Device

The X-ray output from the X-ray generator passes through the object to be photographed and is then converted into a digital signal through the detector to be output to the image signal. X-rays weaken in dense tissue such as bones, and soft tissue uses the principle of easy passage to adjust the brightness of the image to express the signal.

The indirect type detector consists of a Scintillator for converting X-rays into visible light and a TFT panel for converting them into data. A TFT array consists of covered with a thin layer of silicon that is amorphous or disordered, and silicon is engraved with millions of transistors arranged in higher order like a grid of graph paper. Each of these thin-film transistors (TFTs) is attached to a light-absorbing photodiode that makes up individual pixels, and visible light entering the photodiode is converted by intensity into a digital signal and output to the screen in a readable digital image.

Mode of action

AED Mode (Auto Exposure Detection Mode, Not use switch box)

The user can acquire images without additional connection between the X-ray system, which is already installed, and the detector. When X-rays are exposed by an exposure switch of the installed X-ray system, the detector detects the X-rays that are absorbed without any additional external exposure signals and convert them into digital signals.

Converted digital data is delivered to PC via AP (Access Point) or main cable, and PC converts the delivered data into images to show it on the monitor.

※ Object, PC, X-ray System are not included in this component.

Normal Mode (with Switch Box)

The user can acquire images by connecting Switch Box to X-ray system that is already installed. Normal Mode is to send exposure signals to PC and X-ray System using Switch Box and a separate exposure switch. By pressing the exposure switch connected to Switch Box, the detector is activated and ‘Ready’ sign can be seen. When prepared, the detector delivers exposure signals to X-ray System.

The detector converts absorbed x-ray to digital data, delivers it to PC via AP (Access Point) or main cable, and PC converts the delivered digital data into images to show it on the monitor.

※ Object, PC, X-ray System and Control panel are not included in this component.

2

General description

The flat panel sensors of the ADD are fabricated using thin film technology based on amorphous silicon technology. Electronically, the sensors are much like conventional photodiode arrays. Each pixel in the array consists of a light sensing photodiode and a switching Thin Film Transistor (TFT) in the same electronic circuit. Amorphous silicon photodiodes are sensitive to visible light, with a response curve roughly comparable to human vision. The sensitivity of amorphous silicon

photo diodes peaks in green wavelengths, well matched to scintillators such as CsI. The response has the excellent linearity of a charge integrating biased photodiode.

ADD is used for all X-RAY systems, but please check compatibility between ADD and X ray system for use. In addition, it cannot be used for dental and mammography.

3

Intended purpose

ADD, Digital Flat Panel X Ray Detector is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnosis for physicians and radiologists. Not to be used for mammography.
