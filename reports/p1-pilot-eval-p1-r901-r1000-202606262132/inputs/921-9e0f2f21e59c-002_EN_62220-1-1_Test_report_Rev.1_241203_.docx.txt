A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW should be used within the limits of the operating temperature. After the system power is on, 30 minutes of warming up time is required to acquire high quality X-Ray images. X-ray absorber

The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW device uses a CsI screen on a substrate. This scintillator is commercially available and converts x-ray energy directly to light. It has an extremely linear light output response of x-ray exposure for a given x-ray spectrum over the range appropriate for radiography.

The X-ray beam passes through the substrate and interacts with the CsI. The theoretical quantum efficiency of the detector is a function of x-ray energy E and is given by

where αC(E) is the x-ray transmission factor through the carbon substrate and αS(E) is the transmission factor through the screen material.

Energy conversion mechanism

The X-ray photons interacting in the screen are converted directly to light. This light exits from the screen and be absorbed at photodiode arrays.

The photodiode arrays convert light to electrical signals proportional to the intensity of the light. The signal readout circuit makes digital image data from the electrical signals from photodiode arrays.

Readout mechanisms

The detector outputs raw image data to the workstation, via 1Gbps Ethernet or Wireless to Access Point. The acquisition software in the workstation receives the 16-bit digital image data and processes it. It is stored in the hard disk in a 16-bit format.

Output data

Images can be exported in raw image format and saved in a storage device of the workstation. The raw data is after pre-processed data which means just calibrated image (gain-offset correction and bad pixel correction). The saved raw data from A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW will be transferred to the end user’s S/W and then converted to a DICOM or Bitmap file. The size of the raw image output data is editable by using image acquisition software.

2.3.5. Quantify the effects aliasing has on the DQE and Modulation Transfer Function (MTF) of the device. If aliasing is evidenced from the pre-sampled MTF, quantitatively describe how it affects the resulting image. If there are no aliasing effects from the device explain why.

 Generally, the actual effects and extent of aliasing artifact in image is the integrated value under MTF curve above the cut-off frequency (nyquist frequency). The ideal value of MTF at cut-off frequency is zero. However, real measured pre-sampling MTF at cut-off frequency is over zero. The detector element determines the spatial resolution capability of the detector. Its pitch of this system is 0.14 mm corresponding to a sampling cut-off frequency of 3.57 lp/mm. The MTF value at this frequency is approximately close to zero. For average human observers, MTF values around 0.1 are considered barely observable. Therefore, artifacts in the image due to aliasing will be negligible. In addition, no evidence of aliasing was observed in a test pattern image acquired with this detector (shown below). If there are aliasing artifacts, near nyquist frequency, the white and black spokes may reverse - i.e., white bars become black and black become white. This occurs between frequencies 1/(pixels width) and 2/(pixels width), and is one kind of aliasing artifact..

2.3.12. Latent image decay characteristic as a function of time and temperature.

 When the x-ray exposure is ended, the image is read immediately by the TFT imaging sensor and hence no image decay is possible.

 To reduce thermal difference or variation due to surrounding conditions of the TFT imaging sensor, and to maintain the offset at the same level with each image acquired, the dynamic offset correction method is used. It feed-back the offset level to maintain always similar value. There is therefore no significant change due to changes in ambient temperature.

2.3.13. Recovery Time for radiographic devices: Indicate the time after readout is complete (including any preparation function) until the device is able to accept the next exposure.

Readout of the detector after an x-ray exposure requires least 1 second with window 0.5 second. And to stabilize offset 7 seconds are needed. Thus, the minimum time between exposures is 8 seconds.
