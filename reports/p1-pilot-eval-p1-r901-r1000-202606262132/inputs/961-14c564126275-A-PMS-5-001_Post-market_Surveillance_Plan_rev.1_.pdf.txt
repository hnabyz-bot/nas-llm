4. Device Information
4.1 Product Name
Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector
4.2 Model Name
A1717MCW / A1417MCW / F1417MCW
4.3 General Description
The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are wired or wireless digital flat panel detectors that have
been designed for faster, more streamlined approach to digital radiography systems. The detector
consists of TFT and Scintillator (CsI) and an electronic device that converts data read through it into
digital signals. The detectors support an auto exposure detection technology that allows the detectors to
be used without generator integration.
The flat panel sensors of the A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are fabricated using thin film
technology based on amorphous silicon technology. Electronically, the sensors are much like
conventional photodiode arrays. Each pixel in the array consists of a light‐sensing photodiode and a
switching Thin Film Transistor (TFT) in the same electronic circuit. The amorphous silicon photodiode
is sensitive to visual light, and its response curve has almost the same performance as human vision.
The sensitivity of amorphous silicon photodiodes peaks in green wavelengths, well matched to
scintillators such as CsI. It also has excellent linearity according to the exposure dose.
4.4 Intended purpose
The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for
providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is
intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnosis
and treatment planning on a real time basis for physicians and radiologists.

6. Data Assessing Process
1) The data from PMS are analysed whether there are new risks related to the performance and safety
of our devices or similar devices in PSUR (A-PSUR-5-001 Periodic Safety Update Report)
2) If it is related to the risk of our device or similar devices, the data will be evaluated for risk
according to risk management criteria
3) Risk assessment divides severity and probability into 5 stages, records them in the risk management
report, and manages them according to risk management procedures.
Refer to A-TCF-5-103 Risk Management Plan and A-TCF-5-104 Risk Management Report
7. Suitable Indicators and Threshold Values for Benefit-Risk Analysis
Residual risk
There is no known side effect but there are potential risks such as physical damage. Based on the A-TCF-
5-104 Risk Management Report, we collected and analyzed the residual risk and there were residual risks
categorized as probability levels 2 ~ 3 and severity levels 2 ~ 4 however due to the risk control as far as
possible, we reduced the occurrence of probability to improbable or remote (Level 1 ~ 2).
We will collect and research the following clinical data as below:
• Volume of sales including customer complaints, customer survey
• Literature study
• Recall and adverse events of similar devices
• Imaging evaluation
If there is a result on the residual risk that could increase the occurrence of probability to more than level
3, the Benefit/risk ratio should reconsider, and risk management shall be implemented.
If the result shows a decreasing occurrence of probability for residual risk, we can conclude that the
benefit outweighs the residual risk.
Table. Probability levels
Step Probability Definition
5 Frequent More than 1 time / day
4 Probable More than 1 time / month
3 Occasional More than 1 time / 6 months
2 Remote More than 1 time/ year
1 Improbable Less than 1 time / every other year (2 years) or hardly occur

Severity levels Definition

3 Serious Injury or damage that requires professional medical technology

9. Manage the Events of the Trend Report
1) Check if there are any adverse events that cause risks recorded in risk management report among
the data collected through PMS activities. Trend report focus on non-serious incidents and
undesirable side effects. If there is a negative impact on the benefit-risk analysis, monitoring is
carried out.
2) Monitoring collects relevant information over 6 months observation period.
3) Check the ratio of the number of abnormal cases to the number of sales for 6 months. (Number of
occurrences of adverse events / number of units sold) In addition, the severity is checked according
to the risk management procedure.
4) If the analysed likelihood and severity are higher than the previously estimated values, risk
management and CAPA are implemented and trend report is uploaded to EUDAMED and inform
NB.

11. Reference to manufacturers’ obligations
As required within Annex III, Section 1.1 (b) of Regulation (EU) 2017/745, PMS plan shall cover
reference to procedures to fulfil the manufacturers obligations. We have prepared the procedures for Post
Market Surveillance system according to Article 83 Post-market surveillance system of the manufacturer
of Regulation (EU) 2017/745. A-PMS-5-001 Post-market Surveillance Plan is prepared according to
Article 84 Post-market surveillance system of the manufacturer of Regulation (EU) 2017/745 and Article
86 Post-market surveillance system of the manufacturer of Regulation (EU) 2017/745.
12. Procedures to identify and initiate measures
1) Analyse data from PMS according to risk management criteria
2) If it is determined that corrective action is necessary, company reports on corrective and preventive
measures and review by the manager of relevant department.
3) The department requesting corrective action obtains approval from quality manager, and the quality
manager sends it to the department to take action.
4) The department which received the request for corrective action shall investigate the root cause of
the nonconformity.
5) The department to take action establishes a corrective action plan, takes action, and notifies the
requester.
6) The requestor shall check the validity of measures taken. For validation, it is necessary to check
whether corrective and preventive measures have been taken in the quality system, not a specific
individual problem or a one-time measure, and if the measures are insufficient, re-correction is
requested.
7) The requester shall report the confirmed matters to the quality manager.
8) The quality manager terminates the action when a satisfactory result is obtained, otherwise orders
the action again.
13. Tools to trace and identify devices
1) When raw materials are received, a Lot no. is assigned to each raw material.
2) Products that have passed the final inspection are marked with product name, Lot no, manufacturing
date.
3) Manufacturing date, Lot no., Ref. no., quantity and place of shipment are recorded to ensure
traceability.
4) Later, when the UDI system is started, traceability is guaranteed through UDI system.
