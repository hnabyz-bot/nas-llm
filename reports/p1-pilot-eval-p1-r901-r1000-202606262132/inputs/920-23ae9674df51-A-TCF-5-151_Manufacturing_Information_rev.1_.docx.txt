Device Information

Product name

Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector

Model name

A1717MCW / A1417MCW / F1417MCW

General description

The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are wired or wireless digital flat panel detectors that have been designed for faster, more streamlined approach to digital radiography systems. The detector consists of TFT and Scintillator (CsI) and an electronic device that converts data read through it into digital signals. The detectors support an auto exposure detection technology that allows the detectors to be used without generator integration.

The flat panel sensors of the A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are fabricated using thin film technology based on amorphous silicon technology. Electronically, the sensors are much like conventional photodiode arrays. Each pixel in the array consists of a light‐sensing photodiode and a switching Thin Film Transistor (TFT) in the same electronic circuit. The amorphous silicon photodiode is sensitive to visual light, and its response curve has almost the same performance as human vision. The sensitivity of amorphous silicon photodiodes peaks in green wavelengths, well matched to scintillators such as CsI. It also has excellent linearity according to the exposure dose.

Intended purpose

The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnosis and treatment planning on a real time basis for physicians and radiologists.

Principle of operation

The X-ray output from the X-ray generator passes through the object to be photographed and is then converted into a digital signal through the detector to be output to the image signal. X-rays weaken in dense tissue such as bones, and soft tissue uses the principle of easy passage to adjust the brightness of the image to express the signal.

The indirect type detector consists of a Scintillator for converting X-rays into visible light and a TFT panel for converting them into data. A TFT array consists of covered with a thin layer of silicon that is amorphous or disordered, and silicon is engraved with millions of transistors arranged in higher order like a grid of graph paper. Each of these thin-film transistors (TFTs) is attached to a light-absorbing photodiode that makes up individual pixels, and visible light entering the photodiode is converted by intensity into a digital signal and output to the screen in a readable digital image.
