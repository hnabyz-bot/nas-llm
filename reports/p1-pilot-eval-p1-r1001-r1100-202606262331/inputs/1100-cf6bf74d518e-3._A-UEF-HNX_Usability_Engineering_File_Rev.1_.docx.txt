Introduction

Purpose of the Document

This document specifies the usability engineering process for the Portable X-ray System (Model Name: HnX-P1, HnX-PB). It consolidates all activities related to analyzing, specifying, designing, verifying, and validating usability. The purpose is to ensure safety by mitigating risks related to usability problems. This document complies with EN 62366-1:2015.

The variant model(HnX-PB) has only changed the exterior design compared to the existing model HnX-P1, and the interface, exterior material, internal structure, PCB, software, etc. are all the same.

Therefore, no additional usability validation will be conducted for the addition of the variant model(HnX-PB).

Scope

This document covers the usability engineering process, including the usability specification, formative and summative evaluations, risk management, and validation activities for the Portable X-ray System.

Product name: Portable X-ray System

Model name: HnX-P1, HnX-PB

Intended used: The Portable X-ray System is indicated as a digital imaging solution designed for providing the general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adults and children. It is intended to replace film-based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnoses for physicians and radiologists. Not to be used for mammography.

Is perceivable by,

Operating principle

This equipment is a portable X-ray generator for human body imaging, and consists of an X-ray generator that generates X-rays, a high-voltage generator that applies high voltage to the X-ray tube, an X-ray control unit that controls the tube voltage and tube current of the X-ray tube, and an irradiation control unit that controls the area of ​​the X-rays.

When current is applied to the cathode (filament) in the X-ray tube, thermal energy is generated and electrons are emitted. When high voltage is applied to the cathode and anode in the X-ray tube using the high-voltage generator, the emitted electrons are accelerated toward the anode and generate X-rays.

This X-ray outputs X-rays for an area adjusted through the irradiation field control unit to take an image.

Function related to safety

When X-rays are irradiated, the irradiation lamp lights up yellow and a buzzer sounds to alert you to the risk of X-ray exposure.

The hand switch prevents malfunctions during X-ray irradiation.

If you release the 2-stage hand switch button during X-ray irradiation, the irradiation stops immediately.

The X-ray output value is monitored and an error is displayed and the irradiation is stopped immediately if it goes beyond the allowable error.

The irradiation time is detected in real time to minimize excessive exposure during X-ray irradiation and the irradiation is stopped immediately in case of excessive exposure.

The product is protected by automatically cutting off the power in case of overcurrent or overload.

Perform Summative Evaluation of the usability of the user interface

The summative evaluation report contains the following information:

For each hazard-related scenario, a test record be established with:

The hazard-related scenario and alternative evaluated,

The goal of the evaluation,

The user-profile participating to the evaluation,

The environment of evaluation,

The duration of the evaluation,

The IFU provided to the user or no IFU,

The results of the evaluation,

A rationale for setting the result to (i) OK, (ii) not OK, (iii) more information needed, given the goal of the evaluation.

The summative evaluation report ends with a discussion on the overall compliance of the results obtained for all tests. The result of the discussion is one of the three following states:

Compliant:

All tests are OK,

All risks related to usability are acceptable,

No additional data required,

Partially compliant:

At least one test is not OK,

No unacceptable risk is remaining but at least one risk is tolerable,

Additional data shall be collected by PMS and/or PMCF,

Not compliant:

At least one test is not OK,

At least one unacceptable risk is remaining,

Additional studies or design changes are required.

The summative evaluation report is recorded in Usability Validation Report (Doc. No. A-UVR-HNX).
