1. General Description
1.1 Description and Application Scope
This portable radiographic system (Model: HnX-P1) consists of an LCD display with up and down soft-
keys for controlling kV, an X-ray generator (line-powered transformer), an X-ray tube assembly, a
collimator and a cart. It can also use a stand instead of a cart. The HnX-P1 is used with a film-cassette
or flat-panel detector.
This device is a battery-powered portable X-ray System, designed and manufactured by H&abyz.
Compared with traditional X-ray products, this device has exquisite structure, compact design, light
weight and easy operation.
The major components of the X-ray main unit include handle, enclosure, main control panel, system
control board, high-voltage tank, inverter, collimator (beam limiter), and system control software
running on the system control board. The system control software is for real-time interaction and
control with various circuit modules inside the X-ray generator. The software responds to user
operations on the control panel. The user can adjust and control the kV and mAs parameters, and the
software will display the parameters or directly load the APR parameters. The software loads the
control data from X-ray output into the high-voltage generation control circuit of the system control
board, and control the high-voltage tank to generate high-voltage to excite the X-ray tube inside to
emit X-rays, control the switch of the collimator, and monitor the working status of the device, and
control the display of the status indicators.
The system is for X-ray imaging and diagnosis in facilities with portable or fixing sites.
This HnX-P1 is not intended for mammography.
The device can be used with an X-ray flat panel detector, a computer for receiving and detecting
signal results and an image processing software. This portable X-ray System is designed for handheld
or stand-mounted imaging. Model HnX-P1 can be configured to an optional portable stand that
complies with IEC 60601- 1 safety standard. The recommended maximum load that the stand can
safely carry is 6kgs to ensure the mechanical stability and effectiveness of the device.
The cybersecurity risk of the HnX-P1 have been addressed to assure that no new or increased
cybersecurity risks were introduced as a part of device risk analysis. These risks are defined as
sequence of events leading to a hazardous situation, and the control for their risks were treated and
implemented as proposed in the risk analysis (e.g., requirements, verification).

1.8. Notes for using
When using this portable X-ray System, take the following precautions.
Otherwise, problems may occur and the product may not function correctly.
Before exposure
• Be sure to check the product daily and confirm it work properly.
• Sudden heating of the room in cold areas will cause condensation on the product. In this case, wait
until the condensation evaporates before performing an exposure. If it is used when condensation is
formed, problems may occur in the quality of captured images. When an air-conditioner is used, be
sure to raise/lower the temperature gradually to prevent condensation.
• The product should be warmed up for 15 minutes before exposure.
• Do not use the product's X-RAY function while battery charging.
During exposure
• Do not move Power Cable, or it may cause image noise or artifacts, even incorrect images.
• Do not use the product near the equipment generating a strong magnetic field.
Otherwise, it may cause image noise, artifacts or even incorrect images.
Battery Storage
If you are not going to use the X-ray equipment for a long period of time, please remove the batteries
from the X-ray equipment and store them. If the X-ray equipment is stored with the batteries installed
for a long period of time, the batteries may discharge.
Skin Guard
When removing the skin guard from the system, gently lift the hook to remove it. If you lift it with a
strong force, it can be damaged.
Do not lift the skin guard by hand with the skin guard attached to the system body. The skin guard
may be removed and the system may fall.

4.8 Operation Principle
A device that is built by generating high voltage and adding it to both ends of the tube, heating the
bulb filament, shooting the electron cloud of the filament the target of the tube to generate X-rays, and
the irradiation range of X-ray is limited by the collimator.
4.8.1 Operating steps
• The patient is prepped and provided some form of radiation shielding.
• The unit is positioned around the patient, who is asked to remain motionless while the image is being
taken.
• The operator takes the image by activating the X-ray beam using an exposure switch.
• The operator repositions the patient or unit if needed. Multiple images may be taken.
4.8.2 Use and maintenance User(s):
• Surgeon; physician; radiographer or radiology technician; other medical staff
• Maintenance: Biomedical engineering staff and/ or service contract with the manufacturer or third-
party organization
• Training: Initial training by manufacturer, operator’s manuals, user’s guide

5) During indoor radiographic use, remember to keep all bystanders at a minimum safe

6) Please pay attention to the proper management and regular maintenance of this equipment.
7) Cumulative doses of radiation are a serious hazard to patients and users of this device.

requirement for protective devices when dealing with machines producing high kV and mAs
radiation values.

To clean the unit, use distilled water and a soft cloth only.
Do not use coarse cloth, strong chemicals or organic solutions.
Do not use water, benzene, paint thinner or any alcohol base cleaning products. This may

Clean the exterior using a neutral detergent and soft cloth.

the unit.

2. Regular Inspection and Maintenance
In order to ensure the safety of patients and operator, to maintain the performance and reliability of the
device, be sure to perform regular inspection at least once a year. If necessary, clean up the device,
make adjustments or replace consumables such as fuses etc. There may be cases where overhaul is

regular inspection or maintenance.
2.1 General Maintenance
2.1.1 Surface Cleaning
For frequent cleaning, dip the cloth in water or soapy water and wring it dry (water temperature not
exceeding 40℃), and clean the surface of the device and the external cables.
DO NOT use any abrasive cleaner or organic solvent (such material is not allowed) or cleanser with
solvent (gasoline, alcohol or detergent is prohibited).
Do not spray at the device! Never drip any cleanser into the machine!
2.1.2 Disinfection and sterilization
Proper disinfection requires that a disinfectant solution be used. Disinfection of the device should be
performed as needed. Use dampened gauze with 75% medical alcohol and wring it dry to disinfect the
surface of the device and the external cables.

Regular maintenance can ensure that the device runs continuously and safely. Meanwhile with
improved maintenance, damage (such as device downtime and repair) is reduced, and safety
performance (personal injury) is improved.
a) Before turning on the device, make sure the supply voltage is in the allowable range of the device.
b) Turn on/off the device and perform daily operation as described in the operation manual. Do not
attempt any operation in device power-up/power-down process.
Shutting off the power supply directly may cause damage to device hardware and software.
2.3 Regular Maintenance
a) Check the cable and sleeve every 6 months for aging or damage.
b) Check the cable connection of the main components.

If the Portable X-ray System is stored and not for use for over 3 months, some preparatory work is
required to ensure that the Portable X-ray System works properly.
a) Turn on the generator and warm it up for 10 minutes.
b) Train the X-ray tube assembly. Here is the operation instruction: adjust the imaging condition to
50kV/2mAs, and perform exposure once every 2 minutes, 10 exposures in total.
