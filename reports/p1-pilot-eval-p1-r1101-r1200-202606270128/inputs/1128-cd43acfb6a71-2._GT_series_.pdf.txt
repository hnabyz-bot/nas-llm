1.2 Purpose of Use
1.2.1 Intended Use
The GT1717C/GT1717CP Detector is a device that converts medical images into digital format using digital
radiography (DR) equipment and stores and transmits the images. It also includes software.
1.2.2 Patient Group
The GT1717C/GT1717CP Detector is used to examine patients with, or suspected of having, musculoskeletal
injuries and respiratory diseases. Although this product is intended for general patients such as adults and
children, all radiological examinations must be reviewed by the attending physician prior to initiation.
Appropriate techniques, considering the patient's size, should be used to reduce the radiation dose when
acquiring images.
1.2.3 Contra-Indications
This detector cannot be used for mammography.
1.2.4 Features
 This is a Medical Device.
 This product can replace analog radiology diagnosis.
 The scintillator uses CsI.
 You can check the operating status using the button and LED lamp on the side of the detector.
 This detector is designed to be easily transportable.
 You can use the device by connecting it with a LAN cable.
 Type of protection against electric shock: Class I ME Equipment
 Degree of protection against electric shock: N/A (No applied part)

disconnected immediately in an emergency. Obstructions may prevent the
power from being cut off during urgent situations.

cords. To prevent damage to the outer sheath, do not pull, bend, tie, or step on
the cord. Unauthorized modifications are strictly prohibited. Damaged cords
can lead to fire or electric shock accidents.

1.5 Product Operating Guidelines
Follow the product operating guidelines below when using the product. Failure to comply with these
instructions may result in equipment malfunction or damage.
1.5.1 Calibration
 To ensure optimized system performance, it is crucial to verify that the system has been correctly
calibrated.
 Always ensure that calibration has been performed after equipment installation or repair is
complete.
 Do not use the equipment unless the calibration procedure has been completed.
 If manual calibration is difficult, calibration can be performed using the calibration data embedded
in the detector.
1.5.2 Before Acquiring
 Verify and inspect the equipment daily to ensure it is operating normally.
 In cold regions, a sudden increase in indoor temperature may cause condensation inside the
equipment. In this case, wait until the condensation has evaporated completely before proceeding with
imaging. Using the equipment while condensation is present may cause quality issues with the acquired
images.
 When using air conditioning or heating, adjust the temperature gradually to prevent condensation
caused by drastic differences between the room temperature and the equipment temperature.
1.5.3 Acquiring
 This equipment is not provided with special protection (sealing) against the ingress of liquids such as
blood or medications used in operating rooms. If necessary, please use a disposable cover when
operating the equipment.
 Do not use the detector near devices that generate strong magnetic fields. Magnetic field interference
may cause noise or artifacts in the images.
1.5.4 Operating and Storage Environment
 This equipment is primarily designed for use in X-ray rooms and hospital wards. To use it in other
locations, please consult our sales representative or distributor for consultation.
 Do not expose the equipment to high-temperature or high-humidity environments. This may cause
device malfunction.
 When the equipment is not in use, store it in a safe location where there is no risk of falling.
 The environmental conditions specified below must be strictly observed during the operation and

3.2 Detector Connection Methods
The interface utilizing GT1717C / GT1717CP wired detectors and an X-ray generator includes AED and Line Trigger modes.
3.2.1 Connection Modes
AED (Auto Exposure Detection) Mode
In this mode, the detector automatically detects the radiation emitted by the generator without a physical connection.
Therefore, no physical link is required between the detector and the generator.
 Do not subject the product to physical shock. If the product receives a
strong impact, unwanted images may be acquired due to sensor
malfunction even without X-ray exposure.
 Depending on the operating environment—such as exposure conditions,
subject thickness, or the use of grids—images may not be acquired, or
horizontal artifacts may occur.
 When setting the X-ray exposure area toward the detector, the center of
the detector must be included within the exposure area. Otherwise,
image acquisition may fail.
 The object, generator, and PC are not included in this package.
Mode Description
AED The detector automatically detects X-ray exposure from the X-ray generator
to acquire images without the need for a separate cable connection.
Line Trigger
The X-ray generator emits X-rays immediately after sending an exposure
request signal to the detector. The detector then acquires the image. The
X-ray generator and detector must be connected via a generator interface
cable.

A-IFU-CYN(Rev.1) H&abyz Co., Ltd. Page 23 of 44
Line Trigger Mode
The generator and detector are directly connected by a trigger cable. When the generator is ready, it sends a signal to the
detector. Upon receiving the signal, the detector prepares and sends a return signal to the generator, which then emits
radiation.
