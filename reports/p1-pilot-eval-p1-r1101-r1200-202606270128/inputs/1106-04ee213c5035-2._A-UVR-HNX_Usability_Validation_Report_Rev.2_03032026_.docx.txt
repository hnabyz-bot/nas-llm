Introduction

Purpose of the Document

The purpose of this document is to outline the Usability Validation Plan for the Portable X-ray System, Model HnX-P1 & HnX-PB. This plan ensures that the device meets usability requirements, provides a safe and effective user experience, and complies with applicable standards and regulations.

The variant model(HnX-PB) has only changed the exterior design compared to the existing model HnX-P1, and the interface, exterior material, internal structure, PCB, software, etc. are all the same.

Therefore, no additional usability validation will be conducted for the addition of the variant model(HnX-PB).

This device (HnX-P1, HnX-PB) intended use is as follows:

The HnX-P1 (and HnX-PB) is a battery-powered portable X-ray system intended for generating radiographic images of human anatomy.

A usability test is intended to determine the extent an interface facilitates a user’s ability to complete routine tasks. Typically, the test is conducted with a group of potential users with portable equipment. Users are asked to complete a series of routine tasks. Sessions are recorded and analyzed to identify potential areas for improvement to the HnX-P1, HnX-PB.

The usability test objectives are:

To construct the benchmarks of user attributes

To identify potential design problems to be solve

To improve product design and production efficiency

To complete end-user satisfaction maximization

The purpose of the test was to complete the primary operating function described in usability engineering report without the issue regarding to safety and effectiveness.

This document contains the participant feedback, satisfactions ratings, task completion rates, ease or difficulty of completion ratings, time on task, errors, and recommendations for improvements. A copy of the questionnaires is included in the Attachment.

Scope

This report applies to all usability validation activities for the Portable X-ray System, Model HnX-P1, focusing on safety-critical tasks, user interface evaluation, and error minimization.

Summary of Validation Activities

Overview

Validation activities were conducted to evaluate the usability of the HnX-P1 in simulated real-world environments.

Both formative and summative evaluations were carried out to assess the product's safety, effectiveness, and user satisfaction.

Validation Objectives

Verify that the product can be used safely and effectively by intended users.

Identify and mitigate any residual usability risks.

Validation Scope

User Groups: Radiologists and clinicians.

Use Environments: Hospital settings and mobile clinics.

Key Tasks:

Reviewing the user manual and safety warnings.

Preparing the device for use.

Operating the device, including X-ray dose adjustment and image capture.

Cleaning and maintaining the device.

Summative Evaluation Role

The summative evaluation is an integral part of the validation process, providing final confirmation that the device meets usability requirements

Purpose: To evaluate the final design in realistic scenarios with representative users.

Outcomes: No critical usability issues were identified.

Further Improvement

Based the usability verification & evaluation report, some problems are carried out during the usability testing, session of participants interview. Proper risk control measures are required to implement into the design to reduce the derived risks.

Conclusion

It has been shown that the usability engineering activities are performed to full the usability engineering process. Usability verification & evaluation results and improvement action demonstrated that residual risk regarding usability is minimized and acceptable. Since the usability verification & evaluation report demonstrates that the usability goal met the acceptance criteria set in the usability verification & evaluation plan, no evaluation of “benefit outweigh risk” is performed. The re-evaluation of usability will be performed once new use-error issues are raised.
