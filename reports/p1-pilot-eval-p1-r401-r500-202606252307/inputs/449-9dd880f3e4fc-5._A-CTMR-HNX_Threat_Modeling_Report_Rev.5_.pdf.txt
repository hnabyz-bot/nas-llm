1. Overview
1.1 Document Overview
This document aims to provide cybersecurity lifecycle risk analysis and threat modeling for
Portable X-ray System(HnX-P1, HnX-PB).
1.2 Medical Device Overview
- Product name: Portable X-ray System
- Model name: HnX-P1, HnX-PB
- Manufacturer: H&abyz Co., Ltd.
This product is a battery-powered portable X-ray system designed and manufactured by H&abyz.
This portable X-ray system (model: HnX-P1, HnX-PB) consists of an LCD display with up and
down soft keys for kV control, an X-ray generator (power transformer), an X-ray tube assembly,
and a collimator.
Compared to conventional X-ray products, this device boasts a sophisticated structure, compact
design, light weight, and easy operation.
The main components of the X-ray unit are the handle, enclosure, main control panel, system
control board, high-voltage tank, inverter, collimator (beam limiter), and system control software
running on the system control board. The system control software interacts with and controls the
various circuit modules within the X-ray generator in real time. This software responds to user
input from the control panel. Users can adjust and control kV and mAs parameters, and the software
can display these parameters or directly load APR parameters. This software loads control data for
the X-ray output into the high-voltage generation control circuit on the system control board,
controls the high-voltage tank to generate high voltage, excite the internal X-ray tube, and emit X-
rays. It also controls the collimator switch, monitors the device's operating status, and controls the
display of status indicators.
It also allows users to view thumbnails of acquired images on the LCD via Bluetooth.
This product is intended for X-ray imaging and diagnosis in facilities with mobile or fixed locations.
It can be mounted on a cart or stand and used with a film cassette or flat panel detector, a computer
for signal reception and detection, and image processing software. (The cart, stand, film cassette,
flat panel detector, and image processing software are not included in the basic product
components.)
To ensure the mechanical stability and efficiency of the device, the recommended maximum load
that the stand can safely withstand is 6 kg.
The cybersecurity risk of the HnX-P1 & HnX-PB have been addressed to assure that no new or
increased cybersecurity risks were introduced as a part of device risk analysis. These risks are
defined as sequence of events leading to a hazardous situation, and the control for their risks were
treated and implemented as proposed in the risk analysis (e.g., requirements, verification).
The variant model HnX-PB has only changed the exterior design compared to the main model
HnX-P1, and the interface, exterior material, internal structure, PCB, software, etc. are all the same.
Therefore, no additional cybersecurity risk management is performed due to the addition of the
variant model (HnX-PB).

2. Threat modeling Summary
The threat modeling document for the Medical Device HnX-P1 and HnX-PB (Manufacture:
H&abyz Co., Ltd) aims to evaluate potential cybersecurity threats and pinpoint vulnerabilities
within the device. This comprehensive assessment identifies possible attack vectors, evaluates the
likelihood and impact of various threats, and proposes mitigations to enhance the security posture.
By systematically analyzing the device's architecture, data flow, and interfaces, the document
ensures that security measures are robust and aligned with industry standards. In total 16 risks
have been identified during the threat modeling process:
The key findings and outcomes of each test are summarized below:
a) IEC 60601-4-5 Compliance Testing (RT): Ensures medical devices meet requirement for
cybersecurity (35 initial risks)
b) CWE Vulnerability Assessment (VT): Detects and analyzes weaknesses to prevent cyber
threats (56 initial risks)
c) Penetration Testing (PT): Simulates attacks to identify and fix security vulnerabilities in
systems. (4 initial risks)

3. Data-Flow Diagram
A Data-Flow Diagram (DFD) in medical device cybersecurity is a graphical representation that
maps out the flow of data within a medical system. It highlights how data moves between devices,
systems, and users, identifying points where data is input, processed, stored, and output. By
visualizing these flows, DFDs help identify potential vulnerabilities in data handling, transmission,
and storage.
The report's diagram contains the following views: Global System View, Multi-Patient Harm View,
Updateability/Patchability View, and Security Use Case View(s). These views are visually
represented in the diagram and are explained in detail through the accompanying legend. The
legend helps to clarify the purpose and scope of the depicted views in the diagram.

6. Risks Assessment
6.1 Likelihood
Frequent Exploitation is very easy and likely to occur frequently (e.g. public vulnerability)
Very Likely Exploitation is possible and likely to be exploited by malicious attackers
Likely Exploitation is possible under certain conditions, but technically difficult
Unlikely Exploitation is difficult and requires advanced technical knowledge
Very Unlikely Exploitation is practically impossible (e.g. hardware access required)
6.2 Impact
Very High Critical to system safety and possible legal and regulatory violations
High Major device function failure, possible direct risk to patient safety
Medium Possible to degrade system performance or impact data integrity
Low Possible to cause partial function failure or some inconvenience to user experience
Very Low Limitless to system operation
6.3 Risk Level
Critical Extremely hazardous, requiring immediate security action. Potential direct threat to
patient safety.
High High risk, requiring rapid security action. System performance degradation, high
potential for data leakage.
Medium Medium risk, requiring long-term security enhancements but may not be urgent.
Low Low risk, not operationally impactful but may require additional improvements.
Very Low Negligible low risk. Not likely to cause problems in normal operating environments.
