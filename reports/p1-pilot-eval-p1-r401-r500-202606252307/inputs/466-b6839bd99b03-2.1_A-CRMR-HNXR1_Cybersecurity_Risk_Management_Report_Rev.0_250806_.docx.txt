Purpose

The purpose of this report is making sure for sale and usage of the product to satisfy the

safe and expected result considering the intended purpose of HnX-R1 as

preparation of cyber security risk management system in accordance with ISO 14971:2019 ,

TR 24971:2020. After identifying all hazards and deciding the risk based on the

result of decision in severance and occurrence possibility and judging the risk as acceptance

level, and unacceptance level, it reduces hazard with establishing risk reduction measures.

Executing this process continuously, it distinguishes all risks from risk controls, deciding

identified risk and it pursues to remove or reduce risks through the activity of continuous

risk management.

Summary

prior to the release for commercial distribution of the HnX-R1

Risk estimation

After reviewing NIST’s NVD and the CISA KEV Catalog for all off‑the‑shelf and open‑source components in the SBOM, no publicly known vulnerabilities affecting this product were identified.

Subsequently, each hazardous situation was evaluated using the available data (see Chapter 6.1.2 and Step 2 of the Risk Management FMEA table). Risks were then assessed in accordance with the Cybersecurity Risk Management Plan: threats were scored using CVSS, classified into risk levels, and all medium‑and‑higher risks (total of 59) were evaluated.

Risk evaluation

The Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS) method was applied as an evaluation method for cybersecurity vulnerabilities of the Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector (HnX-R1).

Risk reduction

All risks are controlled by adequate risk control measures in accordance with risk management plan. For details, refer to clause 7.1 to 7 .7 in this report.

Risk control option analysis

We use one or more of the following risk control options in priority order listed below, for the risks need to be reduced to an acceptable level.

1) Designing for Inherent safety and manufacture by:

-eliminating a particular hazard,

-reducing the probability of occurrence of the harm, or

-reducing the severity of the harm

with reviewing the national and international standards to meet the requirements and try to reduce the use errors by applying usability engineering process that we endeavor to optimize the user interface in the product.

2) Protective measure in the medical device itself or in the manufacturing process:

-identify hazardous situations in working environment, then adding the adequate measures such as using automatic cut-off safety valves or alarms to alert the user to hazardous conditions,

-adding the risk control measures to prevent the problems possible occurred in production phase in accordance with the quality management plan(production procedures or inspection standard).

3) Information for safety and, where appropriate, training to users

-placing warnings in the labelling of the HnX-R1,

-restricting the use or circumstances of use of the HnX-R1,

-communicating about improper use, hazards that can occur, or other information that help reduce risk,

-promoting use of personal protective equipment, such as X-ray protection clothing and eye-glasses, when operating this equipment,

-including information about measures to reduce harm,

-providing training for users to improve their performance or their capability in detecting errors. or

-specifying necessary maintenance and maintenance intervals, maximum expected product service life, or how to dispose of the HnX-R1 properly.

Evaluation of overall residual risk

After all risk control measures have been implemented and verified, we have gathered and reviewed data and common cybersecurity attacks data to determine whether the medical benefits of the intended use outweigh the overall residual risk.

-If the medical benefits do not outweigh the overall residual risk, then overall residual risk remains unacceptable.

-If the medical benefits outweigh the overall residual risk, then the overall residual risk can be judged acceptable.

-For an overall residual risk that is judged acceptable, we shall decide which information for safety is necessary to disclose the overall residual risk.

The results of evaluation of overall residual risk are as the following;

• Benefits; It helps to diagnose disease in advance via X-ray system.

• Risks; Patient harm from product misbehavior related to cyberattacks.

• Risk Mitigation; It adopted international standards on its design for safety and performance.

• Conclusions; It is verified its safety and performance by various method.

The overall residual risks are determined to be acceptable through cyber security testing and threat modeling, the label, operating method, adverse event reporting and usability expert review. All expert participants and managers were written on Risk management plan.

This overall residual risk evaluation is performed by chief of R&D team and QC team with the knowledge, experience, and authority to perform such tasks.
