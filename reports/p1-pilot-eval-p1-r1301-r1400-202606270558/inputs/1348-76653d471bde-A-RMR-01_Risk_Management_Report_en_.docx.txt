PURPOSE

Introduction

This risk management report is created to investigate potential risk factors on the use of X-ray detector which H&abyz Co.,Ltd.(hereinafter referred as “the organization”) manufactures. The purposes for this report is to figure out the risks, check if they meet requirements of national and/or international requirements and figure out if there are any other risk factors existing so that potential risks are removed. Therefore, the organization is to use this report for the manufacturing of safe products. This risk management report is based on the processes of risk management which means overall processes such as establishment of planning and the results of it shall be remained in this report and if there are any changes made related to modification of technologies on medical device, the analysis shall also be conducted accordingly. The main methods and processes that are selected are based on the regulations of EN ISO 14971:2012.

TERM & DEFINITIONS

Adapted from EN ISO 14971:2012 Cl. 2. Terms and definitions

2.1 Accompanying document: Document accompanying a medical device and containing information for those accountable for the installation, use and maintenance of the medical device, the operator or the user, particularly regarding safety

2.2 Harm: Physical injury or damage to the health of people, or damage to property or the environment

2.3 Hazard: Potential source of harm

2.4 Hazardous situation: Circumstance in which people, property, or the environment are exposed to one or more hazard(s)

2.5 Intended use & Intended purpose: Use for which a product, process or service is intended according to the specifications, instructions and information provided by the manufacturer

2.6 Life-cycle: All phases in the life of a medical device, from the initial conception to final decommissioning and disposal

2.7 Manufacturers: natural or legal person with responsibility for the design, manufacture, packaging, or labelling of a medical device, assembling a system, or adapting a medical device before it is placed on the market or put into service, regardless of whether these operations are carried out by that person or on that person's behalf by a third party

2.8 Medical device: Any instrument, apparatus, implement, machine, appliance, implant, in vitro reagent or calibrator, software, material or other similar or related article, intended by the manufacturer to be used, alone or in combination, for human beings for one or more of the specific purpose(s) of

- diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, treatment or alleviation of disease,

- diagnosis, monitoring, treatment, alleviation of or compensation for an injury,

- investigation, replacement, modification, or support of the anatomy or of a physiological process,

- supporting or sustaining life,

- control of conception,

- disinfection of medical devices,

- providing information for medical purposes by means of in vitro examination of specimens derived from the human body,

and which does not achieve its primary intended action in or on the human body by pharmacological, immunological or metabolic means, but which may be assisted in its function by such means

- aids for disabled/handicapped people,

- devices for the treatment/diagnosis of diseases and injuries in animals,

- accessories for medical devices (see Note 3),

- disinfection substances,

- devices incorporating animal and human tissues which can meet the requirements of the above definition but are subject to different controls.

2.9 Objective evidence: Data supporting the existence or verity of something

2.10 Post-production: Part of the life-cycle of the product after the design has been completed and the medical device has been manufactured

2.11 Procedure: Specified way to carry out an activity or a process

2.12 Process: Set of interrelated or interacting activities which transforms inputs into outputs

2.13 Record: Document stating results achieved or providing evidence of activities performed

2.14 Residual risk: Risk remaining after risk control measures has been taken

2.15 Risk: Combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm

2.16 Risk analysis: Systematic use of available information to identify hazards and to estimate the risk

2.17 Risk assessment: Overall process comprising a risk analysis and a risk evaluation

2.18 Risk control: Process in which decisions are made and measures implemented by which risks are reduced to, or maintained within, specified levels

2.19 Risk estimation: Process used to assign values to the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm

2.20 Risk evaluation: Process of comparing the estimated risk against given risk criteria to determine the acceptability of the risk

2.21 Risk management: Systematic application of management policies, procedures and practices to the tasks of analyzing, evaluating, controlling and monitoring risk

2.22 Risk management file: Set of records and other documents that are produced by risk management

2.23 Safety: Freedom from unacceptable risk

2.24 Severity: Measure of the possible consequences of a hazard

2.25 Top management: Person or group of people who direct(s) and control(s) a manufacturer at the highest level

2.26 Use error: Act or omission of an act that results in a different medical device response than intended by the manufacturer or expected by the user

2.27 Verification: Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence, that specified requirements have been fulfilled

2.28 Biocompatibility: Nature that a raw material to use appropriately fits biological organization of the human body

2.29 Technical agreement: binding contract between two or more parties that assigns responsibilities for technical requirements
