4. Description of the Device

The HnX-R1 wired/wireless digital X-ray imaging system consists of Detector, Power supply box, Battery pack, Battery charger, Other Accessories and software for image acquisition and image processing and does not include the X-ray generator. The detector is a wired/wireless type, and there are 8 types of G series (G1417CW, G1417CWP, G1417FW, G1417FWP, G1717CW, G1717CWP, G1717FW, G1717FWP) and 3 types of 2/3G series (A1717MCW, A1417MCW, F1417MCW). The name of the image acquisition and image processing software (Console software) is HnVUE.

This system is used to capture images by transmitting X-ray to a patient’s body. The X-ray passing through a patient’s body is sent to the detector and then converted into electrical signals. These signals go through the process of amplification and digital data conversion in the signal process device before being sent to the HnVUE (Console Software) and saved in DICOM file, a standard for medical imaging. The captured images are sent to the Picture Archiving & Communication System (PACS) server, and can be used for reading images.

5. Indications for use

The HnX-R1(Digital X-ray Imaging System) is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic system. 

Not to be used for mammography.

1)

Effective Pixel Area

The effective pixel area of the subject device is slightly different from that of the predicate device. This difference results from variations in detector design but does not have a meaningful impact on the overall imaging performance or the clinical usability of the device.

2)

Effective Pixel Number

The effective pixel number of the subject device also shows a minor difference compared to the predicate device. This variation is within an acceptable range and does not affect the diagnostic value of the acquired images.

3), 4)

DQE

The Detective Quantum Efficiency (DQE) is a fundamental physical parameter related to image quality in radiography, representing the efficiency of a detector in converting incident X-ray energy into image signal.

The DQE of the subject device is approximately 10% lower than that of the predicate device at 0.1 lp/mm. However, in accordance with FDA’s Guidance for the Submission of 510(k)s for Solid State X-ray Imaging Devices, clinical images were obtained and reviewed by qualified radiologists, who confirmed that the image quality of the subject device is clinically acceptable.

7. Summary of Non-Clinical Data

Digital Flat Panel X-Ray Detector (HnX-R1) conforms to the voluntary standards such as IEC60601-1, IEC 60601-1-2, IEC 62304, IEC 60601-1-6 and IEC 62366-1. In addition, the FDA’s Guidance for the Submission of 510(k)s for Solid State X-ray Imaging Devices (issued on September 1, 2016) was followed to describe the detector characteristics; Guidance for the Content of Premarket Submissions for Device Software Functions (issued on June 14, 2023) was followed to evaluate the level of concern as basic; Cybersecurity in Medical Devices: Quality System Considerations and Content of Premarket Submissions (issued on September 27, 2023) was also followed to consider issues related to cybersecurity in the design and development process of the device. Load-bearing characteristics and protection against ingress of water were tested and passed. The internal circuit design was demonstrated through EMC emission testing: IEC60601-1-2 and the results were satisfactory. Biocompatibilities were demonstrated through ISO 10993 series to prove the using material safe and effective. Additionally, the risk analysis, necessary verification and validation activities were performed.

8. Summary of Clinical Data

The clinical studies are not required as there are no significant changes that requires assess of the performance made in the subject device, such as new indication, and non-clinical testing demonstrated equivalency to the predicate device sufficiently.

9. Substantial Equivalence

There are no significant differences between the HnX-R1 compared to the predicate device, K223930 and K153401, that would adversely affect their intended use. They are substantially equivalent in terms of indications for use and technological characteristics.

10. Conclusion

The subject device, HnX-R1 is substantially equivalent to the currently marketed predicate device in terms of technical characteristics, design features, operating principles, basis of functional and performance characteristics. Furthermore, the modification does not change to its intended use and indications for use.
