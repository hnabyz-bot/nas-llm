Statement of Confidentiality

The information contained in this Post Market Surveillance Plan is intended for authorized competent authorities and notified bodies of the manufacturer H&abyz Co,.Ltd. It must not be disclosed to third parties without written permission to be given by H&abyz Co,.Ltd. This plan is in compliance with the Helsinki convention.

Introduction

The objective of the Post Market Surveillance (PMS) is to gain and review the experience in the post production phase according to:

MDD 93/42/EEC as amended by 2007/47/EC

MEDDEV 2.12/2 rev 2 Post Market Clinical Follow-Up

MEDDEV 2.7.1 rev.4 Clinical Evaluation

EN ISO 14971:2012 Application of Risk Management to Medical Devices

When establishing and operating the PMS system, the followings should be considered:

Whether or not the product or technology is new,

The extent of available scientific knowledge (e.g., on long term effects),

The state of the art and market experience with similar products and technology.

The Post Market Surveillance (PMS) is the integrated part of quality assurance system, and is based on information received from the field, including complaint monitoring, feedback from sales representatives, reports from regulatory authorities, literature reviews, and service and repair information

Intended Use

ADD(Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector ), Digital Flat Panel X-Ray Detector is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnosis for physicians and radiologists. Not to be used for mammography.

Appendix I. Post Market Clinical Follow-Up (PMCF)

The objective of the Post Market Clinical Follow-Up (PMCF) is to confirm clinical performance and safety throughout the expected lifetime of the medical device, the acceptability of identified risks and to detect emerging risks on the basis of factual evidence.

The decision to conduct PMCF study was based on the identification of possible residual risks and unclarity on long term clinical performance that may impact the benefit/risk ratio.
