1. General Information
1.1 Introduction
This risk analysis is performed on “ADD”, we design and manufacture for the purpose of investigating
the risk of this device. This analysis was planned at the beginning stage of device design, and report is
prepared at the stage of initial lot production. In line with the change of risk and technology on the
device this analysis will be performed together. Main methods and procedures we selected herewith are
focused on EN ISO 14971:2012 and EN 60601-1:2006/A1:2013.
1.2 Device information
Product Name: Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector
Model Name: ADD
1.3 Intended use
ADD(Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector), Digital Flat Panel X-Ray Detector is indicated for digital imaging
solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult
and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more
precise case diagnosis for physicians and radiologists. Not to be used for mammography.
1.4 Overview
The ADD is a portable digital X-ray flat panel wireless detector that can generate Images of any part of
the body. This X-ray imaging system consists of an X-ray detector (an a-Si TFT sensor panel with
scintillator), a power supply box, a battery recharger, switch box, battery packs and a main cable.
ADD can be used for radiographic imaging of human body parts such as head, Chest, spinal, abdominal,
particular, hand, foot, and other internal organs. However, do not use the system for mammography
purposes.
Radiated X-rays through the body is processed to produce radiography by the detector. Converted
electric signal is processed by the signal processor to amplify and digitize data and the result is
transferred to the pc.

4.2.1 ESL (Expected Service Life)
The ADD is designed for a service life of approximately 5 years.
: Life cycle report (A-TCF-5-057)
 Basis
- KMG Series capacitor estimated life of Samyoung Electronics.
http://www.samyoung.co.kr/admincenter/files/prod/KMG.pdf
- Capacitor estimated life calculation
L = L0 x 2^((Tmax - Ta)/10)
L : Estimated life (hour)
L0: Life at rated temperature (hour) = 1,000 Hours
Tmax : Rated Temperature (℃) = 105℃
Ta : Ambient Temperature (℃) = 50℃
L = 1000 x 2^((105 – 50)/10)
- Result (suppose): 45,254.8 Hours
 Frequency of use
- General daily use
: Less than 9 hours per day
- Reasonably foreseeable worst case use scenarios
: Assume continuous use for battery charging (using a day 24 hours)
1 day = 24 hours
1 week = 168 hours 1 week = 7 days
1 month = 720 hours 1 month = 30 days
1 year= 8,760 hours 1 year = 365 days
- ESL: About 5 years
