1.2 Device overview

The HnX-R1 wired/wireless digital X-ray imaging system consists of Detector, Power supply box, Battery pack, Battery charger, Other Accessories and software for image acquisition and image processing and does not include the X-ray generator. The detector is a wired/wireless type, and there are 8 types of G series (G1417CW, G1417CWP, G1417FW, G1417FWP, G1717CW, G1717CWP, G1717FW, G1717FWP) and 3 types of 2/3G series (A1717MCW, A1417MCW, F1417MCW). The name of the image acquisition and image processing software (Console software) is HnVUE.

This system is used to capture images by transmitting X-ray to a patient’s body. The X-ray passing through a patient’s body is sent to the detector and then converted into electrical signals. These signals go through the process of amplification and digital data conversion in the signal process device before being sent to the HnVUE (Console Software) and saved in DICOM file, a standard for medical imaging. The captured images are sent to the Picture Archiving & Communication System (PACS) server, and can be used for reading images.

Indication for Use

The HnX-R1(Digital X-ray Imaging System) is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic system. 

Not to be used for mammography.

2. Device descriptions

It consists of a scintillator to convert X-rays into visible light and an a-Si TFT (or IGZO) panel to convert it into data. The TFT array is made up of a thin layer of silicon in an amorphous or disordered state, and the silicon is imprinted with millions of transistors arranged in a high-order manner like a grid on graph paper. Each of these thin film transistors (TFTs) is attached to a light-absorbing photodiode that constitutes an individual pixel. The visible light entering the photodiode is converted into a digital signal according to its intensity, and the data transmitted to a computer (image processing unit) is stored in the DICOM image format, a medical imaging standard, through image acquisition software (HnVUE). The stored images are transmitted to a PACS (Picture Archiving & Communication System) server by the image acquisition software and used for image interpretation.

3.3 Operating principle

1) AED mode (Automatic Exposure Detection Mode, Not use switch box)

The user can acquire images without additional connection between the X-ray system, which is already installed, and the detector. When X-rays are exposed by an exposure switch of the installed X-ray system, the detector detects the X-rays that are absorbed without any additional external exposure signals and convert them into digital signals.

Converted digital data is delivered to PC via AP (Access Point) or main cable, and PC converts the delivered data into images to show it on the monitor

※ Object, PC, X-ray System are not included in this component.

2) Normal mode (Use switch box)

The user can acquire images by connecting Switch Box to X-ray system that is already installed. Normal Mode is to send exposure signals to PC and X-ray System using Switch Box and a separate exposure switch. By pressing the exposure switch connected to Switch Box, the detector is activated and ‘Ready’ sign can be seen. When prepared, the detector delivers exposure signals to X-ray System.

The detector converts absorbed x-ray to digital data, delivers it to PC via AP (Access Point) or main cable, and PC converts the delivered digital data into images to show it on the monitor.

※ Object, PC, X-ray System and Control panel are not include Connects the gender cable to the PC.d in this component.

Part

Descriptions

1

Common functions module

It communicates with the detector via a network socket communication module. It provides detector hardware control commands, such as system initialization, setting the window time for image acquisition, setting the detector operating mode, and setting the exposure time. It also provides hardware status feedback for the detector.

2

Image acquisition module

It sets event handles for image acquisition, sets data processing events, registers notification processing messages for image acquisition, and stores raw images read from the detector.

3

Calibration module

For proper image calibration, the beam transmission efficiency information for each fiber bundle is crucial. Therefore, the beam transmission efficiency information for each fiber is provided in the calibration dataset. The calibration dataset generates a beam transmission efficiency deviation table for each fiber bundle and applies it to the acquired image to ensure a flat field of view.

4

Pre-Processing module

It performs image processing on the raw image. It compares the image with the background level data contained in the raw image, adjusts the noise reduction level and contrast, and removes defective pixels contained in the raw image.

It also applies and processes the specified parameter settings to apply optimal values ​​when removing noise component data contained in the raw image from the object image.

5

Firmware update module

It provides functions for loading detector hardware firmware information, using the latest firmware, and providing commands for managing detector hardware firmware.

6

xSystem module

The xSystem module manages and controls the detection equipment while communicating with the SDK, and supports reading and writing log data related to the impact sensor.

7

FPGA App module

It supports functions for transmitting messages between the CPU and FPGA, transmitting image acquisition commands to the FPGA, setting the operating mode, and storing and processing image data to be transmitted.

8

System task module

It supports and monitors functions such as Wi-Fi status management, power cable inspection, power button inspection, FPGA F/W erase write support, and network status inspection.

9

Interface module

It supports I2C expansion controller control and power supply communication control for peripheral devices, initializing the impact sensor-related chip, and register read/write functions.

6. Caution and Warnings

6.1 Warnings

1) Follow the operating instructions specified in this manual for the safety of users and patients.

2) Consult a physician before using this device on pregnant women or those with health-related limitations in taking X-rays.

3) Do not connect the detector to anything other than those specified. Failure to do so may result in fire or electric shock.

4) To avoid the risk of electric shock, connect this detector only to a power supply with a protective earth connection.

5) Do not touch the signal input/output components and the patient simultaneously. This may result in electric shock due to current leakage.

6) Any additional equipment connected to the medical electrical equipment must comply with the applicable IEC or ISO standards. Furthermore, all configurations must comply with the requirements for medical electrical systems. When connecting additional medical electrical equipment, the system to be connected must comply with the requirements for medical electrical systems. The Medical Devices Act takes precedence over the above.

7) Equipment connected to the detector and the patient environment must be powered from a medically isolated power source or be medically isolated devices. Equipment powered by uninsulated sources can cause leakage currents to exceed safe levels. Leakage current generated by accessories or devices connected to uninsulated outlets can add to the detector's leakage current.

8) Always check the system and patient's condition while using the detector to ensure they are operating normally. If a problem is found, take appropriate measures, including discontinuing detector operation, as necessary.

9) Do not disassemble or modify the detector, as this may cause fire or electric shock.

10) The detector contains components that can cause electric shock; touching them could result in death or serious injury.

11) Do not strike or drop the detector. Strong impacts can damage the detector, and using the detector without repairs may cause fire or electric shock.

12) Immediately remove the detector's batteries, unplug the AC power cord, and contact the H&abyz customer service team or an authorized dealer in the following cases:

(1) If smoke, an unusual smell, or an abnormal sound is detected. (2) If liquid is spilled on the detector or a metal object enters the hole.

(3) If the detector is dropped and damaged.

13) Error messages that occur during detector use are not directly output by the included software. They are provided in text format for use with higher-level software.

14) For error message content and corrective actions, refer to the relevant higher-level software manual.

15) If the problem cannot be resolved with the higher-level software manual, contact the H&abyz Customer Service Team or an authorized dealer.

16) For safety reasons, always turn off the detector before performing the following inspections. Failure to do so may result in electric shock.

(1) When cleaning the detector, turn off the detector, remove the batteries, and unplug the power cable from the AC outlet.

(2) Do not use flammable chemicals such as benzene for cleaning. Failure to do so may result in a fire.

(3) Wear waterproof gloves to avoid direct contact with IPA (isopropyl alcohol) or other liquids. (4) Detector maintenance must be performed by an authorized service provider. If the problem persists, it may result in fire or electric shock.

17) When using the detector, the image acquisition part with the decorative sheet attached may come into contact with the patient.

18) If a cybersecurity threat is detected, first disconnect the Wi-Fi and LAN connections, turn off the device, and then contact the H&Abiz service team.

[Customer Service Team] Phone: 070-4658-9300, Email: sales@absales@abyzr.com

6.2 General precautions

1) This equipment should only be used by users with specialized medical education and training, such as physicians, radiologists, and other medical professionals.

2) Do not install the detector in locations with the conditions listed below. Failure to do so may result in malfunction, malfunction, fire, or injury.

(1) Near water sources.

(2) Locations subject to direct sunlight.

(3) Near air conditioning or ventilation equipment.

(4) Near heat-generating equipment such as heaters.

(5) Areas susceptible to vibration.

(6) Dusty environments.

(7) Environments containing saline or sulfuric acid.

(8) High temperatures or humidity.

3) Do not store the detector in locations with the conditions listed below.

(1) Locations where the connected cable could be caught on the foot or other body part during use or movement.

(2) Pulling too hard on the cable connected to the detector device may damage the cable, resulting in fire or electric shock.

4) For safe use, avoid prolonged contact with the patient (less than 10 minutes).

(1) Maximum surface temperature: 46°C (at an ambient temperature of 35°C)

5) To prevent infection, wipe the Deco Sheet (PC embossed) with a soft cloth moistened with IPA (isopropyl alcohol).

6) The G series detector is rated IP68 for water and dust resistance according to the international standard (IEC 60259), while the 2/3G series detectors are rated IP56. Water or dust may damage the product, so observe the following precautions when using the product.

(1) If the product gets wet, wipe it with a clean, soft cloth and dry it completely.

(2) Avoid exposure to salt water or deionized water.

(3) Water and dust resistance are only guaranteed when the wired connection cable is removed, the device is connected wirelessly, and the battery cover is properly secured.

(4) The battery cover is a consumable part and should be replaced periodically after 2,500 uses. (3 times a day during the week, estimated lifespan of 3 years).

(5) If the battery cover is not properly secured or loosened due to a fall or impact, the waterproof/dustproof function may not be maintained. Furthermore, if the detector frame is deformed or damaged due to excessive force or impact, the waterproof/dustproof function may not be maintained.

7) For safety reasons, always turn off the power to each device and remove the battery when the detector is not in use.

8) The battery must be used under the conditions listed below. Failure to do so may result in malfunction, malfunction, fire, or injury.

(1) Recharge the battery using the detector or the battery charger provided by H&abyz.

(2) If the battery must be stored for an extended period, remove it from the detector and battery charger and store it in a cool, dry place.

(3) Keep the battery away from objects that generate static electricity when charging, using, or storing it.

(4) If the battery terminals become dirty, wipe them with a dry cloth before use. (5) Use the battery only in the environment specified by H&abyz. Failure to do so may shorten the battery life.

(6) If the battery becomes abnormally hot, malfunctions, discolors, deforms, or exhibits any abnormalities during use, charging, or storage, stop using the battery.

(7) Do not intentionally short the (+) and (-) terminals with metal objects.

(8) Do not touch liquid on the battery surface.

(9) Do not pierce the battery with sharp objects such as needles or screwdrivers.

(10) Do not strike the battery with heavy objects.

(11) Do not step on the battery, throw it on a hard surface, or drop it to avoid mechanical shock.

(12) Do not disassemble the battery or modify the battery design, including the electrical circuitry.

9) Use the battery charger only under the conditions listed below. Failure to do so may result in malfunction, failure, fire, or injury.

(1) Always charge the battery charger with a battery product provided by H&abyz. (2) When using the battery charger, always use the adapter provided by H&abyz.

(3) Always disconnect the power supply and battery pack when moving the battery charger.

(4) Always avoid contact with liquids or foreign matter when using the battery charger.

(5) Do not disassemble the battery or battery charger.

(6) When using the battery charger, always observe the user environment. This may degrade battery performance and shorten its durability.

(7) The battery charger must be used in a control room. This battery charger is not designed for use in a testing laboratory.

(8) If the battery charger will not be used for an extended period of time, always disconnect the battery charger from the power supply.

(9) Be careful of water or foreign matter when installing the battery in the battery charger.

10) Protect all cables from being stepped on, tripped over, damaged, or subjected to stress.

11) For safety reasons, the detector must be inspected before use. In addition, regular inspections should be performed at least once a year. 12) If the detector is defective, do not disassemble it. Maintenance of the detector must be performed by an authorized service provider. Please contact the H&abyz customer service team or an authorized dealer.

13) Check the user manual for replaceable components.

6.3 Precaution for use

1) Connect the cable to the designated connector. Failure to do so may result in malfunction or damage to the detector.

2) Connect the battery to the designated connector. Failure to do so may result in malfunction or damage to the detector.

3) Handle the detector with care, as strong impacts or dropping it may damage it.

4) Use the detector on a flat surface to prevent it from bending. Failure to do so may result in damage.

5) Check the detector daily to ensure it is functioning properly. Sudden heating of a room from a cold location may cause condensation to form on the detector. In this case, wait until the condensation has cleared before performing an exposure. Using the detector with condensation on the detector may result in poor detector quality. When using an air conditioner, gradually raise or lower the temperature to avoid temperature differences between the room and the detector, thereby preventing condensation. Follow the recommended room temperature.

6) Do not use the detector near devices that generate strong magnetic fields. In this case, noise or distortion may occur in the image.

7) Avoid contact between the connector and the patient.

8) The connector is intended for external connection to the device and must comply with IEC standards.

9) Do not apply excessive weight to the detector. Failure to do so may result in damage to the detector.

10) Ensure that the patient's weight is evenly distributed over the detector surface.

11) If the patient's weight must be concentrated in one area, a weight distribution cap must be used.
