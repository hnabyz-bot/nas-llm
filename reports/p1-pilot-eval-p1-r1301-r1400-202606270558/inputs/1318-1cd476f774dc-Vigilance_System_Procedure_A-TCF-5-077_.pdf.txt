1. Purpose
The purpose of this procedure is to systematically and efficiently collect, analyze, and evaluate safety-
related information recognized when handling and using medical devices to avoid health and harm to
patients, users and others by seeking appropriate safety measures and reducing the likelihood of
recurrence of medical accidents.
2. Scope
The medical institution applies to notification and evaluation of safety information management, such
as the occurrence of a medical accident or Field Safety Corrective Action (FSCA) and side effects of a
product manufactured/sold by the company.
3. Applicable standard references
 MDD 93/42/EEC as amended by 2007/47/EC
 MEDDEV 2.12/2 rev 2 Post Market Clinical Follow-Up Studies
 MEDDEV 2.7.1 rev 4 Clinical Evaluation
 EN ISO 14971:2012 Application of Risk Management to Medical Devices
 EN ISO 13485:2016 Quality management systems - Requirements for regulatory purposes
 NB-MED-2_12-1 Rev.11 Post-Marketing Surveillance(PMS)
 MEDDEV 2.12/1 [Jan. 2013 Rev.8] Guidelines on medical device vigilance system
5. Working process
5.1 Reception of an accident
1) The quality assurance department manager or service agent shall immediately notify the
chairman of the quality committee(Quality Management Representative) of the accident and
local or related information of any damage caused by human or properly from the domestic and
overseas government agencies and the users of the agency products.
2) The chairman of the quality committee shall convene the quality committee for the accident. The
quality committee shall analyze the accident details, establish measures. If necessary, an
accident investigation team headed by the QMR or the QM manager can be organised and
dispatched to the accident site.
3) If necessary, AS personnel, relevant department personnel, or accident investigation teams shall
be send to the accident site to investigate the details of the accident and the cause of the
accident shall be investigated.
(1) Serious cases of harms
(2) Unexpected harmful cases
(3) No significant adverse event occurred, but a recurrence could lead to serious adverse event
(4) Cases in which the association with medical devices is not clear but where significant adverse
events have occurred
(5) Harmful cases other than those of No.(1) through No.(4)
(6) Data on measures related to the safety of medical devices by foreign governments

5.2 Incidents to report to government agencies
The quality manager shall report in case of any of the following accident to the local or local
government agency in accordance with the decision or the quality committee.
1) In case of an accident leading to death
2) In case of an accident that has a serious adverse effect on the health of the patient, the user, or
other persons
3) Serious adverse health effects include the following:
(1) Life-threatening illness or injury
(2) Permanent damage to body function or permanent damage to body structure
(3) A condition requiring internal or surgical surgery to prevent permanent damage to the body's
function or permanent damage to the body's structure
4) Accidents that may have had a serious adverse effect on death or health
(1) Accidents involving medical devices that have already occurred
(2) If an accident occurs again, it may cause death or serious physical damage
(3) Information about medical device testing or equipment may include potential accidents such
as accidents that may provide several possible factors(for example, characteristics or
performance variations or lack of information) are referred to as "probable accidents"
5) If the regulatory requirements that apply require an entity to notify about a complaint that meets
the specified reporting criteria for the issue of side effects or recommendations, the
manufacturer shall document the procedure for notifying the applicable regulatory authority.
Records reported to regulatory authorities shall be maintained.
5.3 Evaluation of probability
In assessing the relationship between a product and a likely accident, accident investigation
personnel should consider the following:
1) Opinions of medical professionals based on evidence where possible
2) Results of pre-assessment of accidents reviewed internal company, such as product inspection
records
3) Evidence of previous similar accidents
4) Other evidence collected internal company
(1) Malfunction or degradation of product characteristics and performance
(2) Products that exhibit no malfunction or deterioration, but that have characteristics that can
cause an accident should be reported as probable accidents.

5.4 Incident handling and reporting on export products (Europe)
1) Manufacturer general requirements
(1) The manufacturer or the representative shall report to the National Authority for medical
accidents and Field Safety Corrective Action (FSCA) when it is deemed necessary to write the
report.
(2) The manufacturer is responsible for investigating medical accidents and for discussing the
need for corrective action. It is the manufacturer's responsibility to know the Vigilance System
(VS) provisions, as well as other representatives in the European Economic Zone who are
responsible for selling devices to the market.
(3) The manufacturer shall notify that the medical accident report can be shared by agents in
the European Economic Zone responsible for selling the device to the market and by other
agents operating on the medical device vigilance.
(4) Each manufacturer shall submit a report to the competent authority when the medical
accident resulted from the combined use of a device (or accessory) manufactured by two or
more different manufacturers.
(5) The manufacturer must note that the Notified body(NB) advises the manufacturer of any
problems that may arise during the post-manufacturing phase affecting certification. These
records include related changes from the vigilance system.
2) Medical device reporting system
The manufacturer or their representative shall submit the first medical accident report to the
competent authority for record and evaluation. Each initial medical accident report should be
completed up to the final report unless the first and final report is prepared as one. Medical
accidents that occur outside of the European economic zone and do not lead to Field Safety
Corrective Action(FSCA) are not required to be reported. However, medical accidents leading to
FSCA should be reported in relation to the European economic zone. The criteria for manufacturers
to report to the competent authority are as follows.
(1) The occurrence of the event is as follows, but is not limited to this.
a) Malfunction or deterioration of characteristics or performance Malfunction or deterioration
may be understood as a malfunction of the device for the intended purpose when used in
accordance with the manufacturer's instructions for use.
(2) To evaluate the stage between the device and the medical accident, the manufacturer
explains:
a) Choice based on possible evidence, choice of health care professionals
b) Preliminary assessment results of the manufacturer's own medical accident
c) Evidence of previous, similar medical accidents
d) Other evidence held by the manufacturer

3) Manufacturer’s Periodic Summary Report's requirements
After one or more Initial Reports have been reported and evaluated by the manufacturer, there are
numerous special occasions where the competent authority recognizes periodic summary or trend
reports from the manufacturer or the representative. This report is agreed by manufacturers and
their respective jurisdictions, and continues the forms and frequencies for which they have agreed
on certain device types and events. If the manufacturer receives consent from the competent
authority for the replacement of the Periodic Summary Report (PSR) and trend report, the
manufacturer shall inform other relevant competent authorities of the agreement and its form. The
periodic summary report may only be extended by another competent authority in accordance with
the consent of the competent authority of that country. Medical accidents are described in the Field
Safety Notice. Each medical accident characterized by FSN that occurred after the manufacturer
caused the FSN to be problematic and associated with the FSA need not be reported. Instead,
manufacturers should equally understand the frequency and satisfaction of the PSR with the
competent authority. General and documented incidents of health care that have been assessed for
risk component analysis by manufacturers and jurisdictions may be exempted from reports prepared
by the competent authority and the manufacturer and should be changed by PSR. However, these
medical incidents are observed and trigger levels should be measured. Trigger levels for interim
reporting shall be consulted by the competent authority. The interim report is small if it exceeds the
trigger levels. The PSR may only be extended by other competent authorities when the jurisdiction
of each country agrees.

A defect in a device that is always identified before it is partially missing from the instructions for

(2) An event caused by patient's condition
If the manufacturer provides information on the cause of the event because of the patient's
condition, the event is not required to be reported. This condition is before or during device use.

5) Trend Reporting
A pre-defined trigger levels used to measure the launch of a report, a type of report used by the
manufacturer, that is significantly increased regardless of the medical accident under conditions that
do not require reporting, and that should be reported to the competent authority. For trend reports
to be available, manufacturers need to have a suitable system of scrutiny in order to take action in
advance for the propensity of client complaints and medical accidents that may be caused by
related devices. Trend reports of manufacturers or representatives and relevant local authority are
necessary when the following are rapidly increasing: Trend reports should be made regardless of
what PSR agrees with.
(1) Medical accidents already reported
(2) Medical accidents with no reporting at all times
(3) Events not always reported

to in the considerations for managing exceptional use shall be evaluated by the manufacturer. The
assessment is determined by risk management, available technicians, design verification, corrective
and preventive action processes.

7) Details included in manufacturer's report
Compose the required details of medical accidents contained in reports reported to agents by
manufacturers, agents, or persons responsible for being sold to the market. Reports by electronic
tools(e-mail, online information system, xml, etc.) are also effectively important. If an initial report
is prepared by voice tools(e.g., telephone), it should be documented by the manufacturer or his
representative as soon as possible. The report should also include an effective description of the
report, which is prepared by the manufacturer without bias and which does not include an approval
for liability for the consequences of medical or medical accidents.
8) Initial reporting period for medical accidents
In general, the report should be written about the competent authorities of the country in which the
medical accident occurred.
(1) Serious public health threat
Report the measure within two days without delay, even if it is not defined after it has been
recognised by the manufacturer.
(2) Death or unanticipated serious deterioration in state of health)
Report immediately after the manufacturer establishes the relationship between the device and
the event, if not defined within 10 days of the recognition of the accident.
(3) Others
Report the relationship between the device and the event, even if not defined within 30 days
from the date the manufacturer recognises the accident.
(4) If the recognition of a reported medical accident occurs after the incident is reportable but is
still possible, the manufacturer must produce a report according to the type of medical accident
required. All reporting times are referred on the first report by the competent authority. The

relevant interface is available from the Committee's website.

(1) the principle of investigation
While the competent authority confirms the progress, the manufacturer shall conduct the
investigation normally and shall define the final report according to the time limit. If the
manufacturer cannot conduct an investigation on a medical accident, it must notify the
competent authority immediately. The competent authority may intervene or initiate an
independent investigation. This shall be negotiated with the manufacturer.
(2) Data acquisition for medical devices expected to occur
The manufacturer may advise the user of each medical incident before reporting to the
competent authority. The manufacturer also needs access to the expected equipment associated
with the medical accident for the purpose of determining whether a medical accident should be
reported to the competent authority. In such cases, the manufacturer shall make reasonable
efforts to collect data on the device and, if possible, request assistance from the competent
authority to obtain the data from the device in order to make measurements. Delay can result
from the loss of evidence in the future analysis of an impossible root cause. For example, short-
term loss of memory stored in device software, and the degree of exposure to blood, is the loss
of evidence. The manufacturer then notifies the competent authority before following the
methods. If the manufacturer has collected data on the device, initial data acquisition (the stage
of confirmation or decontamination) shall include the action of the device in a way that may
affect the following analysis.
10) Survey results and additional results
(1) Survey results and additional results principle
The manufacturer shall include advice from the competent authority as necessary and shall take
action in accordance with the investigation conducted by the FSCA. The competent authority
shall be deemed appropriate and shall take further action as advised by the manufacturer.
(2) Follow-Up Report
The manufacturer shall provide additional reports to the competent authority when the
investigation period reaches the time limit for submitting the initial report to the competent
authority.
(3) Final Report
This is the final report prepared in the survey results and the action stage. Actions include:
- No action
- Post-review of the device in use
- Corrective action for future production
- FSCA
The final report is carried out by the manufacturer with the competent authority that submitted
the initial report. After the competent authority has carried out the survey, the manufacturer
may inform the results.

- Device/ Serial/ Lot/ Batch number range
- Explain that if the lot and lot areas are taken, the other device will not be affected
- Certification of manufacturer and representative(address, contact)

respondent of his support should inform the equivalent competent authority of the FSCA already

include information about the validity of measures taken in countries such as the recall
percentage of the device. The manufacturer shall provide a draft of the FSN to the relevant
competent authority, such as the area in which the device is operated by the manufacturer or
their representative in relation to the market or other competent authority. In general, if the
FSCA is a short time limit, such as a severe public health threat, the manufacturer must pay the
FSN's response within 48 hours. It is recommended that FSN be communicated to the
designated agency involved in the device compliance assessment process.

5.5 Protection of reporter, etc.
A person in charge of collecting, analyzing, and evaluating safety information such as side effects, or
an employee's personal information is admitted to violate the person's personal information, such as
the person's life or personal information.
5.6 Preservation of records
The relevant data (such as corrective data) for the life of the product (at least five years, more than
two years after the product's release) shall be reported and processed by the 'Regulations for
Management of Safety Information such as Medical Devices' and MEDDEV 2.12/1 rev.8. Guideline
on a Medical Device Vigilance System.
