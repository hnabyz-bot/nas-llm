1. Purpose
This clinical imaging evaluation is intended to demonstrate that the Portable X-ray system
(INNOVISION-P5) is clinically adequate for its intended use. The clinical images were
acquired from the subject device and the evaluated by a qualified expert (licensed
radiologist).
2. Reference
• Section VII Clinical Considerations of “Guidance for the Submission of 510(k)s for Solid
State X-ray Imaging Devices.”
3. Device (INNOVISION-P5) Information
3.1 Intended Use
This is a portable X-ray system with following limitations of use: The device may be used
for handheld diagnostic imaging of body extremities. The device may be used for stand
mounted diagnostic imaging of head, abdomen, or extremities. The device may be used for
stand mounted imaging of the chest when used without a grid.
- Not to be used on bariatric patients, unless imaging body extremities
- Not for mammography use
- This device is not intended to replace a stationary radiographic system, which may be
required for full optimization of image quality and radiation exposure for different exam
types.

* Rationale for Patient(Subject) Selection
: X-ray does not differ in image quality or output characteristics by race, and the results may
vary depending on the individual physical characteristics of the clinical subject, such as body
thickness and BMI index. The selection of Patient (Subject) of this product includes people with
a standard body type (BMI 20-30) who satisfy the indication for use, and additionally, the
Indication for Use of the product generally specifies bariatric patients defined as BMI 30 or
higher as the exclusion target, but considering the worst case, we selected subjects (BMI 30-40)
in the range of up to BMI40, which corresponds to the 95th percentile of adult male BMI, as the
worst case and performed a clinical evaluation on thick areas (abdomen, chest, skull, shoulder).

4.4 Criteria of Image Evaluation
The investigator was instructed to evaluate the clarity and diagnostic quality of anatomical
features visible in the images using the softcopy display. The anatomical areas evaluated were
selected based on their importance for diagnostic image interpretation, as determined by the
investigator.
The investigator rated the clarity of anatomical features using a scale from 1 to 4: 1(Grade C)
being the lowest score, 2(Grade B) indicating average clarity, and 4(Grade S) representing the
highest clarity. A score of 4 was assigned when the target feature was clearly visible and the
image quality was excellent. A score of 3 was given for images better than average, while a
score of 2 indicated that the target was visible but of average quality. A score of 1 indicated that
the feature was not visible due to poor image quality.
Grade Value Means
Grade S Perfect = 4 Very good image and clearly visible
Grade A Excellent = 3 Better than average image
Grade B Average =2 Average image, target is visible
Grade C Poor =1 Poor image, target is not visible

7. Conclusion
The images acquired using the INNOVISION-P5 were analyzed and evaluated for image
quality.
The key factors in image quality, contrast, spatial resolution, and density, were determined to
be at an excellent level.
In particular, bone imaging demonstrated clear differentiation between soft tissue and bone,
with well-defined bone structures that allow for easy distinction.
For thicker chest images, potential image degradation due to scatter radiation was considered;
however, the anatomical structures were clearly delineated.
Additionally, the image quality in the chest region, which requires precise differentiation of
subtle variations, was assessed as excellent.
For thin structures such as hands and feet, the distal phalanges were adequately visualized, and
the differentiation between bones and joints was distinctly observed.
The images acquired using the INNOVISION-P5 were concluded to provide sufficient image
quality and diagnostic capability to meet the requirements of medical imaging.
This device meets the diagnostic radiography standards by producing high-resolution and detailed
images. Furthermore, it has been confirmed that this device effectively supports diagnostic purposes
and delivers reliable performance in clinical applications.
