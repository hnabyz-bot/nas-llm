1. Purpose

This clinical image report is intended to demonstrate that the Retrofit Kit of X-ray System (HnX-R1) is clinically adequate for its intended use and is substantially equivalent to the predicate device. For this purpose, clinical image pairs were acquired using both the subject device and a 510(k) cleared predicate device. These images were evaluated and compared by a qualified expert (licensed radiologist) to demonstrate that the subject device provides images of equivalent diagnostic

2. Reference

Section VII Clinical Considerations of “Guidance for the Submission of 510(k)s for Solid State X-ray Imaging Devices.”

3. Device information

3.1 Device overview

The HnX-R1 wired/wireless digital X-ray imaging system consists of Detector, Power supply box, Battery pack, Battery charger, Other Accessories and software for image acquisition and image processing and does not include the X-ray generator. The detector is a wired/wireless type, and there are 8 types of G series (G1417CW, G1417CWP, G1417FW, G1417FWP, G1717CW, G1717CWP, G1717FW, G1717FWP) and 3 types of 2/3G series (A1717MCW, A1417MCW, F1417MCW). The name of the image acquisition and image processing software (Console software) is HnVUE.

This system is used to capture images by transmitting X-ray to a patient’s body. The X-ray passing through a patient’s body is sent to the detector and then converted into electrical signals. These signals go through the process of amplification and digital data conversion in the signal process device before being sent to the HnVUE (Console Software) and saved in DICOM file, a standard for medical imaging. The captured images are sent to the Picture Archiving & Communication System (PACS) server, and can be used for reading images.

3.2 Indication for Use

The HnX-R1(Digital X-ray Imaging System) is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic system.

Not to be used for mammography.

3) Performance

(1) Key Software Features

a. Functions for acquiring, storing, and transmitting medical images

b. Functions for storing and viewing patient information, imaging order information, and images

c. Image manipulation functions, such as zooming in and out, rotating, moving, and adjusting brightness

d. Functions for selecting image layouts, displaying thumbnail images, and zooming in on specific areas

e. Image formats, transmission, and storage supported by the DICOM 3.0 standard

(2) Security Features

a. The imaging software can only be operated by logging in with an authorized user ID. Information and data are protected by preventing access to data by unauthorized users.

b. Image deletion is performed through an authorization process to prevent accidental or intentional deletion.

c. Provides solutions and solutions when the PACS server is down and image transmission is impossible.

d. In the event of a hardware or software error, a notification message is displayed on the screen.

e. Patient information in the captured images can be checked.

f. The device can be used through a communication connection with PACS.
