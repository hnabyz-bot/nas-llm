c) Differences between Subject and Predicate Devices
The technological characteristics of the subject device are the same as the predicate device in most
parts. The difference parts are described in the below table. There are no significant differences
between the subject device and the predicate device that would adversely affect the use of the
product. These differences do not raise different questions of safety and effectiveness than the
predicate.
No Similar or Differences Explanation
1 Weight, Size
Differences in this regard do not affect the safety and
effectiveness of the product to achieve its Indications for
Use.
2 Energy Source
(Rechargeable Battery)
Although there is a difference in the Energy Source
(Rechargeable Battery), the safety of the battery of the subject
device has been proven by the IEC 62133-2 laboratory test.
Therefore, it does not have a negative impact on the safety and
effectiveness of the device related to the indications for use.
3 Exposure times
The exposure times are slightly different between the predicate
device and the subject device, but it does not negatively affect
the safety and effectiveness of the device related to the
indications for use.
4 mA
The mA of the subject device is lower than that of the
predication device, but the validity of the indication for use was
confirmed through clinical images reviewed by a qualified
clinician.
5 Memory settings
(technique)
The memory setting function of the predicate device can store
up to 5 values, and the subject device can store up to 10
settings through APR mode. This is a function for the
convenience of the user and does not have a negative impact
on the safety and effectiveness of the device.
6 kW
The kW of the subject device is slightly lower than that of the
predicate device. To ensure the validity of this, we filmed a total
of 30 clinical image samples of limbs, including elbows, hands,
knees, ankles, and feet, and reviewed them by a qualified
clinician. It was confirmed that the differences in this area did
not negatively affect the safety and effectiveness of the device.
7 kVp
The kVp range of the subject device is included in the kVp range
of the predicate device. Therefore, the differences in this area
did not negatively affect the safety and effectiveness of the
device.
8 X-ray Tube, Collimator
Although the X-ray Tube and Collimator are different, they have
been verified through IEC 60601-2-28 and IEC 60601-2-54 test
lab tests. Therefore, the differences in this area do not have a
negative impact on the safety and effectiveness of the device.
