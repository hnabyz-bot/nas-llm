1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose of the Document
This document specifies the usability engineering process for the Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector
(Model: GT1717C, GT1717CP). It consolidates all activities related to analyzing, specifying, designing,
verifying, and validating usability. The purpose is to ensure safety by mitigating risks related to usability
problems. This document complies with IEC 62366-1:2015.
Variant model differences
: The two products differ only in CsI deposition thickness, as shown below, and are identical in
usability.
- GT1717C : 300um
- GT1717CP : 500um
1.2 Scope
This document covers the usability engineering process, including the usability specification, formative and
summative evaluations, risk management, and validation activities for the Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector.
· Product name: Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector
· Model name: GT1717C, GT1717CP
· Intended used: The Digital X-ray Imaging System is indicated for digital imaging solution
designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and
children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic system.
Not to be used for mammography..

3.1.2 Risk Control as it relates to User Interface design
To reduce use-related risk, the manufacturer shall use one or more of the following options, in the
priority listed (as required by ISO 14971:2019, 7.1):
a) Inherently safe design and manufacture;
b) Protective measures in the medical device itself or in the manufacturing process; and
c) Information for safety and, where appropriate, training to users.
3.1.3 Information for Safety as it relates to Usability
When in accordance with the priorities of 4.1.2, information for safety is used as a risk control
measure, the manufacturer shall subject this information to the usability engineering process to
determine that the information
- Is perceivable by,
- Is understandable to, and
- Supports correct use of the medical device by
Users of the intended user profiles in the context of the intended use environment.
Conscious disregard of such information for safety by the user is considered to be an intentional
act or intentional omission of an act that is counter to or violates normal use and is also beyond
any further reasonable means of user interface-related risk control by the manufacturer (i.e.
abnormal use).
Compliance is checked by inspection of the information for Safety and the Usability Engineering
File.
3.1.4 Usability Engineering File
The results of the Usability Engineering Process shall be stored in the Usability Engineering File. The
records and other documents that form the Usability Engineering File may form part of other documents
and files.

4.1.6 Operating principle
This is a wired digital flat panel x-ray detector, a faster and more efficient digital radiography
system. The detector is used in combination with a TFT glass and scintillator and supports
automatic trigger signal detection technology that can be used without generator integration.
The incident X-rays are converted into visible light that produces electron hole pairs in a
photometer biased by scintillator material. The charge carrier is stored in the photodiode
capacity. By pulse processing the gates of the TFT lines in the matrix, charges in all the columns
are transferred in parallel with the signal output. All signals on the column are amplified by a
custom Read Out IC for further processing.
The amplified signal is digitized using an A/D converter integrated into the ROIC. The digitized
signal is transmitted to the PC.

4.6 Perform Summative Evaluation of the usability
The summative evaluation report contains the following information:
For each hazard-related scenario, a test record be established with:
· The hazard-related scenario and alternative evaluated,
· The goal of the evaluation,
· The user-profile participating to the evaluation,

· The environment of evaluation,
· The duration of the evaluation,
· The IFU provided to the user or no IFU,
· The results of the evaluation,
· A rationale for setting the result to (i) OK, (ii) not OK, (iii) more information needed, given the
goal of the evaluation.
The summative evaluation report ends with a discussion on the overall compliance of the results obtained
for all tests. The result of the discussion is one of the three following states:
· Compliant:
o All tests are OK,
o All risks related to usability are acceptable,
o No additional data required,
· Partially compliant:
o At least one test is not OK,
o No unacceptable risk is remaining but at least one risk is tolerable,
o Additional data shall be collected by PMS and/or PMCF,
· Not compliant:
o At least one test is not OK,
o At least one unacceptable risk is remaining,
o Additional studies or design changes are required.
The summative evaluation report is recorded in Usability Validation Report (Doc. No. A-UVR-CYN).
