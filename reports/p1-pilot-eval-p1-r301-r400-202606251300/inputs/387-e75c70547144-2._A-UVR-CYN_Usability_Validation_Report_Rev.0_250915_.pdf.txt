1 Introduction
1.1 Purpose of the Document
The purpose of this document is to outline the Usability Validation Plan for the Digital Flat Panel X-ray
Detector, Model GT1717C/GT1717CP. This plan ensures that the device meets usability requirements,
provides a safe and effective user experience, and complies with applicable standards and regulations.
Variant model differences
: The two products differ only in CsI deposition thickness, as shown below, and are identical in usability.
- GT1717C : 300um
- GT1717CP : 500um
This device intended use is as follows:
The Digital X-ray Imaging System is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general
radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film
based radiographic diagnostic system. Not to be used for mammography.
A usability test is intended to determine the extent an interface facilitates a user’s ability to complete routine
tasks. Typically, the test is conducted with a group of potential users with portable equipment. Users are asked
to complete a series of routine tasks. Sessions are recorded and analyzed to identify potential areas for
improvement to the Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector (Ref no. GT1717C, GT1717CP).
The usability test objectives are:
• To construct the benchmarks of user attributes
• To identify potential design problems to be solve
• To improve product design and production efficiency
• To complete end-user satisfaction maximization
The purpose of the test was to complete the primary operating function described in usability engineering report
without the issue regarding to safety and effectiveness.
This document contains the participant feedback, satisfactions ratings, task completion rates, ease or difficulty
of completion ratings, time on task, errors, and recommendations for improvements. A copy of the
questionnaires is included in the Attachment.
1.2 Scope
This report applies to all usability validation activities for the Portable X-ray System, focusing on safety-critical
tasks, user interface evaluation, and error minimization.

2 Summary of Validation Activities
2.1 Overview
• Validation activities were conducted to evaluate the usability of the Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector (Ref
no. GT1717C, GT1717CP) in simulated real-world environments.
• Both formative and summative evaluations were carried out to assess the product's safety, effectiveness, and
user satisfaction.
2.2 Validation Objectives
• Verify that the product can be used safely and effectively by intended users.
• Identify and mitigate any residual usability risks.
2.3 Validation Scope
• User Groups: Radiologists and clinicians.
• Use Environments: Hospital settings and mobile clinics.
• Key Tasks:
- Reviewing the user manual and safety warnings.
- Preparing the device for use.
- Operating the device, including X-ray dose adjustment and image capture.
- Cleaning and maintaining the device.
2.4 Summative Evaluation Role
• The summative evaluation is an integral part of the validation process, providing final confirmation that the
device meets usability requirements
• Purpose: To evaluate the final design in realistic scenarios with representative users.
• Outcomes: No critical usability issues were identified.

7 Conclusion
It has been shown that the usability engineering activities are performed to full the usability engineering process.

the usability goal met the acceptance criteria set in the usability verification & evaluation plan, no evaluation
of “benefit outweigh risk” is performed. The re-evaluation of usability will be performed once new use-error
issues are raised.
