1. Overview and Purpose
1.1 Overview
As the development of medical devices increasingly utilize communication technology, medical
devices are becoming more prone to attacks such as data leaks, data manipulation, and hacking
that threaten the devices’ security. These cybersecurity threats can cause errors or defects on the
software and pose potential risks to the end users. To better manage these risks and increase the
safety and performance of Aspen Imaging’s medical devices, this risk management report will outline
and establish a risk management system according to cybersecurity standards to identify and reduce
any potential hazards of AspenView Software manufactured by Aspen Imaging Healthcare Inc
(hereinafter referred to as “we”).
1.2 Purpose
This risk management report is intended to first satisfy regulatory requirements for medical
image acquisition devices developed, produced and sold by our company. We identify all risk
factors (hazard) to alleviate cyber security threats such as hacking of medical devices and
information leakage of products manufactured by our company by establishing a risk
management system according to the cybersecurity application of medical devices. We will
determine the severity and risk based on it. After determining the risk as an acceptable or
unacceptable area, risk reduction measures will be established to reduce those risks.

3.9 Expected Service Life
• Software expected service life is virtually unlimited. However, a software program is expected to
perform no more than 10 years due to compatibility issue that may be enforced by the change of
operating system that runs the program. The acquisition program is usually affected by
Workstation (especially, the changes of window OS).
Therefore, the expected service life of our device has been determined to be approximately 10
years based on the window OS update cycle. However, the actual life of the device may be shorter
than 10 years due to the frequency of use and the hardware life expectancy. And it does not apply
to the system damaged due to the negligence of the user.

7.3 Category of Mitigation Measure
For risk mitigation measures that have been implemented in 7.2, this section will outline the
different categories of controls that have been put in place as a result of those mitigation
measures.
7.3.1 Authentication Controls
o Authentication controls have been significantly strengthened for AspenView by increasing
the complexity of password requirement, locking users out after repeated trying to login
unsuccessfully, and ensuring that a single account can only have a single session logged
into the software.
7.3.2 Authorization Controls
o Authorization controls have been reinforced to ensure that the software integrity and the
data integrity can be maintained. Updates to the software can only be made by an
administrator, and an admin can only patch the software with a set of files that need to be
validated by a password from the manufacturer.
7.3.3 Cryptography Controls
o Cryptography controls of AspenView have been improved to ensure that the sensitive
data that the program handles are safe from external threats. All patient data is encrypted
using DICOM standards, and credentials are encrypted with SHA-256 method to ensure
decryption cannot happen without manufacturer’s decryption methods.

12. Medical Device Cybersecurity Requirements and Essentials Checklist
1) Definition and Classification of Cybersecurity Safety for Medical Devices
Safety level and requirements of cybersecurity in medical devices with wire and wireless
communications can be different depending on the level of harm which can be caused by
potential defects from cybersecurity violations and affect to users.
The cybersecurity safety level for medical devices can be divided into three as shown in the
table 1 below: major, moderate and minor.
Level ‘Major’ refers to the case where medical device cybersecurity breaches could cause
serious injury or death, permanent impairment of body function or possibility of permanent
damage to body structure. Level ‘Moderate’ refers to the case where users suffer from
temporary and minor injuries and need medical intervention. Level ‘Minor’ refers to the case
where users experience temporary discomfort or suffer from reversible, minor and short-
term discomfort without the need for medical intervention.
[Table 1. Classification of Cybersecurity Safety Level for Medical Devices]
Classification Definition
Major
Medical device cybersecurity breaches could cause serious injury or death,
permanent impairment of body function and possibility of permanent damage
to body structure.
Moderate Medical device cybersecurity breaches could cause temporary and minor
injury to users and medical intervention may be necessary.
Minor
Medical device cybersecurity breaches could cause temporary discomfort or
reversible, minor and short-term discomfort to users without the need for
medical intervention
