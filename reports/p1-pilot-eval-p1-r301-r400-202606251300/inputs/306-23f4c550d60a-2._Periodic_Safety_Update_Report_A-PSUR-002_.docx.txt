0. Introduction

General

Manufacturers of class IIa, class IIb, and class III devices shall prepare a periodic safety update report in accordance with the article 86 of the Regulation (EU) 2017/745 (MDR).

This is a report for the Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector to summarise results and conclusions of the analyses of the post-market surveillance data gathered as a result of the post-market surveillance plan referred to the Regulation (EU) 2017/745 together with a rationale and description of any preventive and corrective actions taken.

Related references

PSUR submission system for each class

For class III devices or implantable devices, manufacturers shall submit PSURs by means of the electronic system referred to in Article 92 of MDR to the notified body involved in the conformity assessment in accordance with Article 52. The notified body shall review the report and add its evaluation to that electronic system with details of any action taken. Such PSURs and the evaluation by the notified body shall be made available to competent authorities through that electronic system.

For devices other than those, manufacturers shall make PSURs available to the notified body involved in the conformity assessment and, upon request, to competent authorities.

Limits for start and end dates of each search: February 23, 2021(Initial CE marking) ~ December 15, 2022

The selection/criteria

Searched literature or documents which is identical or similar to search condition below after collecting data by using search term, should be selected for appraisal of clinical data.

Condition of Data Selection:

(1) Inclusion Criteria

 The literatures addressing any of the topics listed below were included:

 - The literature is generated from the actual use of the equivalent devices

 (DRX-1, CXDI – 31, CXDI-70C, CXDI-11 and etc.).

 - The literature is related to Digital Flat Panel X-ray detector.

 - The literature addresses the usefulness of the Digital Flat Panel X-ray detector.

 - The literature addresses the safety of Digital Flat Panel X-ray detector.

 - The literature addresses the performance of Digital Flat Panel X-ray detector.

(2) Exclusion Criteria

 The literatures addressing any of the exclusion criteria listed below were excluded:

 - The literature is not related to Digital Flat Panel X-ray detector.

 - The literature doesn’t address the safety and performance of the Digital Flat Panel X-ray

detector.

 - The Digital Flat Panel X-ray detector is not the main focus of the literature.

 - There is a newer or a more complete study about the same research.

 - The full text is not available.

For more information on the literature searches performed and the results generated, you can refer to the Literature Search Report (Document No. A-CLSR-01).

Information, including feedbacks and complaints, provided by users, distributors and imports

Feedbacks

None

Complaints

A total of 3 customer complaints occurred, and the details are as follows.

DATA CABLE fastening bolt is broken

: Poor customer handling, bolt replacement, specification change to one with stronger bolt strength.

Bottom plate bolt fastening micro loosening

: Estimation of loosening of the use process by the user's mobile, changing the torque value of the electric screwdriver.

Poor contact of Power Box LAN Cable

: Power Box LAN Cable contact terminal pin recession (defective raw material), additional monitoring is planned by reflecting the relevant items in the inspection items.

There were no matters that had a significant impact on the safety or performance of the product or required design changes.
