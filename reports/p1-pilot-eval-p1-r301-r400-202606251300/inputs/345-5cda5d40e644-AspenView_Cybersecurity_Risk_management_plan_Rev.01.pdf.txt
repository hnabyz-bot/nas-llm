5. Risk management applied to product life
Risk management is applied to the overall procedures from planning and development of
product to production and service.
◼ Design & Development Planning
At the design and development planning stage, conduct research for the intended use and
safety of the product. Research center, sales department, service and quality assurance
department find the risk examples of similar products and investigate the market. The
manufacture makes a decision for the development through opened discussion.
◼ Design & Development Input
At the design and development input stage, the intended use, performance, safety and legal
requirements as well as international standards and requirements should be considered. The
risk factors expected in normal and abnormal condition shall be considered especially. The risk
management plan shall be in line with the development planning made through reviewing by
mechanical part manager, electrical part manager, software part manager in research center
and QA manager, In accordance with development procedure, design specifications shall be
decided. The risk management report will be made in accordance with risk management
procedure.
◼ Design & Development Output
Design and development output shall control the risk factors analyzed in design and
development input, Design and development output shall be in line with design specification in
accordance with development procedure, It also include the drawings, part list, manufacturing
process, quality management planning and manual. Design and development output documents
shall include the intended use of product, safety, customer service and installation.
◼ Design & Development Review
Design and development review decides whether the residual risk is acceptable or not. Design
and development review will be held by development meeting for the new product and renew
the risk management repot. It also reviews the design input and output documents as well as
practicality and reliability for the product.

◼ Design & Development Verification
At design and development verification, the process in which the adequate test is implemented
shall include the objective reporting. In accordance with product evaluation manual, conduct an
evaluation for developments and check the risk control measures. If there are still risks, do
analyze them and reflect on designing. At the time, evaluate whether the risks are acceptable
◼ Design & Development Validation
Design and development validation as a final development procedure check whether the
product is adequate to the intended use and customer’s requirements. It also checks whether
the residual risks are acceptable. We conduct the verification for a prototype in accordance with
product evaluation manual. If risks are found, we take the measures against the risks and
conduct a residual risk evaluation. QA takes a reliability test and transfer the developments to
production line.
◼ Product Transfer
If the development is approved to be completed, research department enrolls the design
document and distributes them in accordance with document management procedure.
Production department joins in manufacturing of prototype at development stage by which they
acquire the technology and experience the manufacturing prior to product transfer.
◼ Control of Design Changes
If the intended use or production process is changed, if the problems are found in use, it is
possible to revise the product. Design changes shall be conducted for performance increase,
cost down, production increase in accordance with design change manual.
The department requested design changes submits the design changes proposal to research
center. The developer who is engaged in product development checks the risks and safety. If it
is judged not to be harmed, the technical documents shall be distributed with approval of chief
researcher.
◼ Traceability
Traceability management is to clear the traceability of material, part and product, by which
manufacture take a quick measure against the claim.
Traceability management is conducted in accordance with identification and traceability
procedure, inadequate product management procedure.
The all products at ex-factory shall be through the risk control based on product test report and
secured with traceability.

◼ Purchasing Control
Purchasing control is managing the delivery date, quality and price of the import product in
accordance with purchase management procedure. If there is a design change requirement for
the import part from supplier, it is applied to the product through risk evaluation and safety
evaluation from research department.
Production & Process Control
Manufacture control and check the production process to secure the good quality in accordance
with production and inspection equipment management procedure.
◼ Post Product
For the manufactured product, take an examination for the product in accordance with measure
and inspection management procedure. If the inadequate product is detected, take an action in
accordance with inadequate product management procedure and correction and prevention
procedure. The qualified product is in and out in accordance with part and product management
procedure and delivered to customer.
◼ Post Management
The product delivered to customer shall be installed to qualify intended use in accordance with
installation process manual and give a training in accordance with service procedure.
Manufacture assigns the service worker to take a quick action against customer’s claim. Sales
staff and service staff visit regularly.

8. Acceptability of risk
Hazard is defined as the below.
※ Hazard = Severity X Probability
8.1 Criteria for possibility judgment
Severity Score Possible description
Critical 9.0~10.0 Results in patient death
High 7.0~8.9 Result in injury or impairment requiring professional
medical intervention
Medium 4.0~6.9 Results in temporary injury or impairment not
requiring professional medical intervention
Low 0.1~3.9 Inconvenience or temporary discomfort
8.2 Acceptability of risk
8.2.1 Three categories of risk
Risk is classified as following 2 categories.
◼ Green Zone : Acceptable zone
It is set as an acceptable risk zone, in which risks in this zone do not need risk control.
◼ Red Zone : Unacceptable zone
It is an unacceptable zone, in which case of product development is needed, and should be
reduced to acceptable zone as low as possible through risk control for risk reduction.
If residual risk still exist in an intolerable zone even after the elimination or reduction of risks, the
manufacturer may gather and review data and literature to determine if the medical benefits of
the intended use outweigh the residual risk.
