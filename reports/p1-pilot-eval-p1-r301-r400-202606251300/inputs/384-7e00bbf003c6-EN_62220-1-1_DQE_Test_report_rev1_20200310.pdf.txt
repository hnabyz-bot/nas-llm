2.3.5. Quantify the effects aliasing has on the DQE and Modulation Transfer Function (MTF) of the
device. If aliasing is evidenced from the pre-sampled MTF, quantitatively describe how it affects the
resulting image. If there are no aliasing effects from the device explain why.
- Generally, the actual effects and extent of aliasing artifact in image is the integrated value
under MTF curve above the cut-off frequency (nyquist frequency). The ideal value of MTF at cut-
off frequency is zero. However, real measured pre-sampling MTF at cut-off frequency is over
zero.
The detector element determines the spatial resolution capability of the detector. Its pitch of
this system is 0.14 mm corresponding to a sampling cut-off frequency of 3.57 lp/mm. The MTF
value at this frequency is approximately close to zero. For average human observers, MTF values
around 0.1 are considered barely observable. Therefore, artifacts in the image due to aliasing will
be negligible.
In addition, no evidence of aliasing was observed in a test pattern image acquired with this
detector (shown below). If there is aliasing artifacts, near nyquist frequency, the white and black
spokes may reverse - ie, white bars become black and black become white. This occurs between
frequencies 1/(pixels width) and 2/(pixels width), and is one kind of aliasing artifact..
