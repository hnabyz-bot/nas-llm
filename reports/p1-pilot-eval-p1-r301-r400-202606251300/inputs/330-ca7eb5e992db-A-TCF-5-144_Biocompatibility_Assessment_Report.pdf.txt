1. Introduction
This study is planned for the selection of Biocompatibility test for HnX-R1. The purpose of this assessment is to
ensure that no undue risks arise from biological hazards associated with materials of manufacture and final
devices.
2. Product Description
2.1 Product overview
The HnX-R1 wired/wireless digital X-ray imaging system consists of Detector, Power supply box, Battery pack,
Battery charger, Other Accessories and software for image acquisition and image processing and does not
include the X-ray generator. The detector is a wired/wireless type, and there are 8 types of G series (G1417CW,
G1417CWP, G1417FW, G1417FWP, G1717CW, G1717CWP, G1717FW, G1717FWP) and 3 types of 2/3G
series (A1717MCW, A1417MCW, F1417MCW). The name of the image acquisition and image processing
software (Console software) is HnVUE.
This system is used to capture images by transmitting X-ray to a patient’s body. The X-ray passing through a
patient’s body is sent to the detector and then converted into electrical signals. These signals go through the
process of amplification and digital data conversion in the signal process device before being sent to the HnVUE
(Console Software) and saved in DICOM file, a standard for medical imaging. The captured images are sent to
the Picture Archiving & Communication System (PACS) server, and can be used for reading images.
a) Common name: Digital X-ray Imaging System
b) Model name: HnX-R1
c) Manufacturer: H&abyz Co., Ltd.
d) Indication for User: It is intended to replace film-based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide
more precise case diagnoses for physicians and radiologists.
