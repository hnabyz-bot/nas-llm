5. Performance and Test
5.1 Test Sampling and environmental condition
5.1.1 Test sample condition
The battery used for the test shall be manufactured and delivered no later than one month before.
5.1.2 Environmental condition
The test shall be performed at 23±2℃ and 65±15%RH
5.1.3 Test equipment condition
The grade of voltmeter and ammeter used in the test shall be higher than class 0.5, a high Impedance
type.
5.2 Standard test conditions
5.2.1 Standard charge
“Standard charge” means charging the pack with a charge current 0.2C5A and constant voltage
12.60V till charge current is less than 0.02C
5.2.2 Standard discharge
“Standard discharge” means charging the pack with discharge current 670mA with 9.0V Cut-off
at 23℃Within 1hour after the standard charge.
5.3 General Performance
5.3.1 Initial capacity
Perform the standard charge and discharge cycling for 3 times, and measure the last Discharge
capacity
*The initial capacity shall be higher than Minimum capacity (Min. Capacity is 85% nominal Capacity)
5.3.2 Cycle life
After 300 cycles of standard charge and discharge as below conditions, the pack is
Measured for the duration time
*each charge and discharge step include 10minutes rest time
*Last discharge capacity after 300th cycle shall be higher than 70% minimum capacity
5.3.3 Discharge capacity with temperature
The means relative value of discharge capacity at various temperature compared with the standard
Discharge capacity as 100%
*Pack is charge by standard charge condition.
*Pack is discharge by standard discharge condition with various temperature.
Relative capacity 30% 87% 87%
At -10℃ 23℃ 40℃
5.3.4 Storage
The fully charged pack by the standard charge condition was stored at 25±2℃ for 30 day
And the capacity be measured by the standard discharge condition.
*Remaining Capacity shall be higher than 50% of the minimum capacity

5.4 Safety Performance
5.4.1 Overcharge test
Test method: Apply charge voltage 15V to the pack.
Criteria: PCM shall operate and stop charging
NO damage such as leakage, flame, or fire was allowed.
5.4.2 Eternal short circuit test
Test method: Short-circuit the standard charged pack by connecting positive and Negative
Terminals with 50Ω wire for 1 hour.
Criteria: PCM shall operate and stop charging
NO damage such as leakage, flame, or fire was allowed.
5.4.3 Over discharge test
Test method: Discharge the pack to less than 7.50V
Criteria: PCM shall operate and stop charging
NO damage such as leakage, flame, or fire was allowed.
5.5 Mechanical Performance
5.5.1 Vibration Test
Test method: This means the endurance of the pack against vibration.
Frequency and amplitude : 10Hz→ 30Hz, 0.38mm ; 30Hz→ 55Hz , 0.19mm
Sweep speed : 1 ± 0.055Hz/min
Criteria: NO damage such as leakage, flame, or fire was allowed.

[Appendix A]
1. General
Battery packs supplied by mPLUS Electronics Co., Ltd. have to be handle carefully according to the
specification. Here are some more to be followed.
2. Storage of pack
The packs are requested to be stored under the following conditions:
a. Indoor storage in a cool circumstances without direct sun light on the packs or cartons.
b. Store batteries in a dry location with low humidity, and a temperature range of - 20°C to +30°C. In case
of the long term storage
a. As long-term storage can accelerate battery self-discharge and lead to the deactivation of the batteries.
To minimize the deactivation effect, store battery packs in a temperature range of +10°C to +30°C.
b. When charging for the first time after long-term storage, deactivation of the packs may have led to
decreased capacity. Recover such packs to original performance through repeating several cycles of
full charging and discharging.
c. When store packs for more than 6 month, charge at least once charring require per 6 months to
prevent leakage and deterioration in performance due to self-discharging.
3. Charging pack
a. Use suitable charger with the specified voltage and current. We strongly recommend the smart battery
charger. We can recommend the usage or specification of the charger manufacturing.
If you want to get the information about it, please contact us.
b. Never attempt reverse charging. Charring with polarity reversed can cause a reversal in battery polarity,
causing gas pressure inside of the battery to rise, which can be lead to leakage of the batteries in the
pack.
c. Avoid overcharging. Repeated overcharging can be lead to deterioration in pack performance. And
Over-heat occurred.
d. Charging efficiency drops at temperatures above 40°C.
4. Protection from unexpected damaged to pack
a. (+) and/or (-) terminals must not be connected in metal wire, necklace, chains.
b. Do not drop packs from height in order to prevent them from possible malfunction or damage.
c. Do not twist or bend packs in order to prevent possible damage.
5. For Safety
a. Do not disassemble packs.
b. Do not use pack when something abnormal found such as smells, deformation, discoloration, and so on.
c. Do not re-use Li-ion cells or other parts after removing from the packs.
d. When the electrolyte leakage occurs, do not touch the liquid.
e. Once watered, packs may have potential malfunctions. Do not use those packs.
f. Do not have packs in the hot-temperature (60°C or more).
g. Do not put packs into fire.

6. Disposal guidance of battery
a. Don't throw away your batteries, separate them with recycled items.
