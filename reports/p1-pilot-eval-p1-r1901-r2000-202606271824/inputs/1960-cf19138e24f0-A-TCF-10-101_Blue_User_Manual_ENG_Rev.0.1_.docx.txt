Purpose of Use

Intended Use

G1417/G1717 Series are digital flat panel detectors that is used for screening and diagnosis of disease of injury. These detectors are intended for use by a qualified/trained doctor or technician on both adult subjects for taking diagnostic radiographic exposures of the skull, spinal column, chest, abdomen, extremities, and other body parts.

Patient group

G1417/G1717 Series is used to examine in patients with or suspected of muscle and bone injury, respiratory diseases. It is intended for general patients such as adults, children, and infants, but all radiography should be reviewed by a doctor in charge prior to beginning the examination.

The radiologist should use a proper technique considering the patient’s size to decrease the radiation dose when he or she acquires diagnostic images.

Contra-Indication

These detectors are not intended to be used for mammography applications.

Features

This is a Medical Device.

Since G1417/G1717 Series detectors are compatible with a conventional film cassette, they enable to replace the analog radiographic diagnosis.

The scintillator provides CsI.

G1417/G1717 Series detectors support IEEE 802.11a/b/g/n/ac to acquire images without a wired connection in anytime, anywhere.

Checks the connecting status of the detector and battery remains through OLED Display.

Makes direct wireless communication with the built-in wireless AP function.

Makes quick application of various functions with two buttons on the side of the detector.

Supports the stable and reliable AED (Auto Exposure Detection) function.

Designed for easy carrying

You can use the product with cable connection (tether interface cable) depending on the using environment.

Type of protection against electrical shock: Class l or Internal powered ME equipment

Degree of protection against electrical shock: Class B applied part

3.2 Detector connection method

You can organize wired or wireless connection between the G1417/G1717 Series detector and Router. You can also choose other connection ways suitable for the use environment in case of need

3.2.1 wired connection method

Directly connected to a PC (workstation) via Lan cable.

G1417 / G1717 Series detector clearly indicates that it is not possible to connect with the generator.

3.2.2 Soft AP connection method

Detector AP mode is a method of wirelessly communicating with a PC using a detector as an AP without a separate AP.

#Detector & PC (Workstation)

The PC and G1417/G1717 Series detectors are connected wirelessly. In this case, the detector operates as an AP (access point) and the PC operates as a station.

By default, the detector is powered by a battery. However, when the detector is connected to the AC-DC adapter, it receives power from the adapter and the battery charges quickly

3.2.3 Wireless connection method

The G1417/G1717 Series detector is connected to a PC (workstation) through a separate router and transmits images and data to the PC (workstation).

Operation

This chapter provides instructions on how to prepare the detector for operation.

basic detector operation

Basic detector operation4.2

Routine Operation

Stating up

On the control panel, users can press the power button to turn on.

When one or more batteries are connected to the detector or a wired cable is connected, the detector automatically turns on.

When the detector is powered down, the user presses the button for 4 seconds to turn on the detector if the battery is inserted and the capacity is not less than 15%, or DC power is connected.

Check whether the OLED is powered.

When removing the battery, turn it off using the power button before removing the battery.

It is recommended to use the button to turn off the detector.

Battery change

G1417 / G1717 series detectors support ‘Hot Swap’ functionality.

Pull on the protruding part of the cover to remove the battery cover.

Install the battery according to the direction of the pins. At this time, no force should be applied to the battery cover or battery.

Put the battery cover on.

# G1417 / G1717 series detectors operate with only one battery.

This is a 'Hot swap' function that does not turn off the Detector power when replacing the battery.

Please follow the steps below when installing the battery cover.

Lift the battery cover to about a 45-degree angle and secure the hooks at the bottom into the grooves of the battery compartment.

When securing the hooks, be careful not to let them get caught on the rubber sealing.

After closing the cover, press lightly on the non-fixed parts to secure it.

Cleaning

Clean the detector with 70% IPA (Isopropyl-alcohol) when it is contaminated.

Before cleaning the detector, turn off the power and separate the battery.

Wear waterproof gloves to protect your hands from direct contact with IPA or any other liquid.

Do not pour or spray IPA directly on the detector. Use fabric or soft cloth moistened with IPA to clean.

Avoid getting IPA or any other liquid into the detector.

After cleaning, wait until the IPA is dried completely.
