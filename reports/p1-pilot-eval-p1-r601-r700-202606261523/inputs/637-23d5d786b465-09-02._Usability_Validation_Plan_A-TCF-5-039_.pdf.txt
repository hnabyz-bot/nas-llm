1 Document Overview
This document describes a test plan for conducting a usability test during the development of ADD. The
goals of usability testing include establishing a baseline of user performance, establishing and validating
user performance measures, and identifying potential design concerns to be addressed in order to
improve the efficiency, productivity, and end-user satisfaction.
The usability test objectives are:
 To construct the benchmarks of user attributes
 To identify potential design problems to be solve
 To improve product design and production efficiency
 To complete end-user satisfaction maximization
2 Methodology
2.1 Participants
The participants' responsibilities will be to attempt to complete a set of representative usability
scenarios presented to them in as efficient and timely a manner as possible, and to provide
feedback regarding the usability and acceptability of the user interface. The participants will be
directed to provide honest opinions regarding the usability of the application, and to participate in
post-session subjective questionnaires and debriefing.
A total 7 participants should be at least equal to the following User Profile used in the Usability test.
a) Education: At least two year of university, equal or more
b) Knowledge: User who meet the legal qualification for each country
c) Language understanding: User who are understand the English
d) Experience: Not require any special experience
e) To allow a disability:
- Mild reading eyesight impairment or corrected eyesight on log MAR 0.2(6/10 or
20/32)
- Equipment along the one touch or grasp the arm / hand
- Average degree of age-related short-term memory damage
- 40% damage due to normal hearing ranging from 500Hz to 2kHz.
2.2 Training
The participants will receive and overview of the usability test procedure, equipment and software.
2.3 Procedure
Participants will take part in the usability test at the testing lab. The ADD will be used in a typical
operating environment described in usability engineering report. The participant’s interaction with
the ADD will be monitored by the facilitator seated in the same place. Note takers and data logger(s)
will monitor the sessions in observation room.

2.4 Roles
The roles involved in a usability test are as follows. An individual may play multiple roles and tests
may not require all roles.
Ethics
All persons involved with the usability test are required to adhere to the following ethical guidelines:
The performance of any test participant must not be individually attributable. Individual participant's
name should not be used in reference outside the testing session.
A description of the participant's performance should not be reported.

3 Usability Metrics
Usability metrics refers to user performance measured against specific performance goals
necessary to satisfy usability requirements. Scenario completion success rates, adherence to
dialog scripts, error rates, and subjective evaluations will be used. Time-to-completion of scenarios
will also be collected.
3.1 Scenarios Completion
Each scenario will require, or request, that the participant obtains or inputs specific data that would
be used in course of a typical task. The scenario is completed when the participant indicates the
scenario's goal has been obtained (whether successfully or unsuccessfully) or the participant
requests and receives sufficient guidance as to warrant scoring the scenario as a critical error.
 Rating: The number of errors occurring (EA)
3.2 Errors Classification
Critical Errors
In general, Critical errors are unresolved errors during the process of completing the task or errors
that produce an incorrect outcome regarding the safety. Also, critical error is that participants may
not be aware that the task goal is incorrect or incomplete.
Critical errors can also be assigned when the participant initiates (or attempts to initiate) and action
that will result in the goal state becoming unobtainable.
Non-critical Errors
In general, Non-critical errors are an error that would not have an impact on the final output of the
task but would result in the task being completed less efficiently.

Non-critical errors are errors that are recovered from by the participant or, if not detected, do not
result in processing problems or unexpected results. Although non-critical errors can be undetected
by the participant, when they are detected they are generally frustrating to the participant.
These errors may be procedural, in which the participant does not complete a scenario in the most
optimal means (e.g., excessive steps and keystrokes). These errors may also be errors of
confusion (ex., initially selecting the wrong function, using a user-interface control incorrectly such
as attempting to edit an un-editable field).
 Rating: The number of errors occurring (EA)
3.3 Subjective Evaluations
Subjective opinions about specific tasks, time to perform each task, features, and functionality will
be surveyed and collected via questionnaires. At the end of the test, participants will rate their
satisfaction with the overall system. These data are used to assess attitudes of the participants.
The questionnaires will utilize free-form responses and rating scales.

4 Usability Tasks (Test scenarios)
The usability tasks were derived from test scenarios developed from use cases and/or with
the assistance of a subject-matter expert. Due to the range and extent of functionality
provided in the ADD, and the short time for which each participant will be available, the tasks
are the most common and relatively complex of available functions. The tasks are identical
for all participants of a given user role in the study.
The task descriptions below are required to be reviewed by the application owner, business-
process owner, development owner, and/or deployment manager to ensure that the content,
format, and presentation are representative of real use and substantially evaluate the total
application. Their acceptance is to be documented prior to usability test.
4.1 Use scenarios related to the primary operating functions.
1. Familiarize yourself with the user manual before and after the product
2. Connecting the power cord to the power supply
3. Connecting the Data Cable to the Power Supply and the Detector
4. Connecting the LAN cable to the power supply
5. Trigger cable connection to power supply
6. LAN cable and PC main body connection
7. Connecting the communication cable to the power supply
8. Power on the detector
9. Arrange the patient in the correct position to have detector aligned with the target body
part
10. Calibration of detector by S/W in PC

4.2 Use scenarios related to the reasonably foreseeable worst case
Identification Sequence of Events /
Tasks as Performed by a Specified User
1. During Normal Use
- Wiring disconnection to Detector => no reading
- Not attention to Detector’s ready before X-ray exposures => no reading
or inappropriate image result
2. Use Errors
- Wrong wiring of interface cable from X-ray machine to Detector
- Dropping
- Applying excessive force
- Wrong calibration in the control software
3. Environment - High temperature => failure of function
- Water/humidity => failure of electronic/electric shock
4. Patient - Positioning by holding a Detector
5. Reading and manipulation - Selection of mode before taking x-ray images
- Set up network configuration for use
** Task Process
1) Explain the activities to user.
2) Take rest for 30min.
3) After activities, record the results to ‘usability validation evaluation’ by interview.
4) Records by check type.
5) Validator judges according to the acceptance criteria.
6) Validator let the non-compatible items to the acceptance criteria be applied to user
interface design and redesigned parts shall be re-validated by above process.

6 Usability Goals
6.1 Completion Rate

Completion rate is the percentage of test participants who successfully complete the task without
critical errors. A critical error is defined as an error that results in an incorrect or incomplete
outcome. In other words, the completion rate represents the percentage of participants who, when
they are finished with the specified task, have an "output" that is correct. Note: If a participant
requires assistance in order to achieve a correct output then the task will be scored as a critical
error and the overall completion rate for the task will be affected.
 Usability Goal: A total completion rate of 100 % is the goal for each usability task.
6.2 Number of Errors
The number of errors include the critical or non-critical errors occurring during the usability task
according to use scenarios. A non-critical error is an error that would not have an impact on the
final output of the task but would result in the task being completed less efficiently.
 Usability Goal: A total errors of below 4 is the goal for each usability task.

7 Reporting Results
The Usability verification & evaluation report will be provided at the conclusion of the usability test.
It will consist of a report and/or a presentation of the results; evaluate the usability metrics against
the pre-approved goals, subjective evaluations, and specific usability problems and
recommendations for resolution. The recommendations will be categorically sized by development
to aid in implementation strategy.
