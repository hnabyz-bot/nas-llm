1. Purpose
The purpose of this regulation is to identify, estimate, and evaluate risk
factors throughout the entire product lifecycle to ensure that medical devices
designed and manufactured by our company are safe for their intended use
and meet expected performance requirements, and to control these risk
factors and reduce the risk level to an acceptable level.
2. Applied Area
This procedure applies to the overall process of managing product risks
associated with the use of medical devices and the risks to patients, users,
other equipment, and the surrounding environment.

5. Work procedures
5.1 Risk Management Process
5.1.1 We establish and maintain a process for proactively identifying hazards
related to medical devices throughout their product lifecycle, assessing,
evaluating, and controlling associated risks, and monitoring the effectiveness
of these controls. The following elements are included in the monitoring
process, and data on monitoring is analyzed in accordance with the Data
Analysis Standard (AQP808).
1) analysis
2) evaluation
3) control
4) production or post-production information
5.2 Management Responsibilities
5.2.1 The Quality Manager demonstrates his/her commitment to the risk
management process by.
1) Providing appropriate resources
2) Assigning qualified personnel to risk management
3) Determining policies for establishing risk tolerance
4) Reviewing the adequacy of the risk management process at established
intervals to ensure the ongoing effectiveness of risk management and
recording evidence of any decisions or activities.
5.3 Qualification of Personnel
5.3.1 Maintain qualification records in accordance with the Human Resources
Qualification Regulations (AQP602).

5.4 Risk Management Plan
5.4.1 The development team leader establishes and records a risk management
plan in accordance with the risk management process. The risk management
plan becomes part of the risk management file and includes at least the
following: Related activities are recorded in the Risk Management Plan (701-
1).
1) As the scope of the plan for risk management activities, identify and
describe the medical devices to which each element of the plan applies
and each stage of its life cycle
2) Responsibilities and authority must be clear
3) Reflect the review requirements of risk management activities
4) Evaluation of verification activities
5) Activities to collect and review related production and post-production
information
6) A matrix that shows which combinations of the likelihood of occurrence of
a hazard and the severity of the hazard in the risk management report are
acceptable and which are not
7) Further refine the matrix (e.g., negligible, tolerable with minimal risk) and
require that the risk be reduced to a reasonably feasible level before the
risk is deemed to be tolerable
8) The criteria for risk tolerance are determined in accordance with our
policy and are based on applicable national or regional laws and
international standards, and refer to available information such as
generally accepted state-of-the-art technologies and the interests of the
parties involved
9) If the risk management plan changes during the medical device life cycle,
a record of the change must be maintained in the risk management file

5.5 Risk Management File
5.5.1 The development team leader establishes a risk management report (701-3)
and manages the following traceability for each identified risk factor.
1) Risk analysis
2) Risk assessment
3) Implementation and verification of risk control measures
4) Determination of the acceptability of any residual risks
5.6 Risk Analysis
5.6.1 Risk Analysis Process
1) Risk analysis is conducted on all medical devices manufactured by our
company. The execution and results of the planned risk analysis are
recorded in the Risk Management Report (701-3).
2) Documentation of the risk analysis and its results shall include at least the
following.
① Description and identification of the analyzed medical device
② Identification of the person and organization that performed the risk
analysis
③ Scope and date of the risk analysis
5.6.2 Identification of characteristics relevant to the intended use and safety of the
medical device
1) Detail the intended use and reasonably foreseeable use errors for the
medical device under consideration. Identify and document qualitative
and quantitative characteristics that may impact the safety of the medical
device, along with appropriate defined limits.
5.6.3 Identification of Hazards
1) The development team leader documents and maintains in a risk
management file the known and predictable risk factors of the medical
device in normal and malfunctioning states.

5.6.4 Risk assessment in each hazardous situation
1) Consider a series of reasonably foreseeable events or combinations
thereof that could result in a hazardous situation, and record the resulting
hazardous situation.
2) For each identified hazard, assess the associated risk using available
information and data. For hazards where the likelihood of occurrence
cannot be assessed, list the possible outcomes for use in risk assessment
and risk control. The results of these activities are recorded in the risk
management file.
3) The system used to qualitatively or quantitatively classify the likelihood of
occurrence of a hazard or the severity of the hazard is recorded in the risk
management plan (701-1) and risk management report (701-3).
4) Information or data required for risk assessment can be obtained from
the following sources.
① Published standards
② Scientific and technical data
③ Field data from similar medical devices already in use, including
reported case reports
④ Usability engineering using typical users
⑤ Clinical evidence
⑥ Results of relevant clinical trials
⑦ Expert opinion
⑧ External quality assessment plan
5.7 Risk Evaluation
5.7.1 For each identified hazard, determine whether the risk is acceptable using
the criteria defined in the Risk Management Plan (701-1). If risk reduction is
unnecessary, these requirements do not apply..
5.7.2 Risk assessment includes an evaluation of factors that may affect product
quality, including risks and environmental factors that may occur during the
process.

5.8 Risk Control
5.8.1 Risk Reduction
1) If risk reduction is required, perform the risk control activities described in
Sections 5.8.2 through 5.8.7.
5.8.2 Risk Control Option Analysis
1) The development team leader identifies appropriate risk control measures
to reduce the risk to an acceptable level.
2) The development team leader uses one or more of the risk control
alternatives listed below in order of priority.
① Inherent safety by design
② Precautions taken during the medical device itself or manufacturing
process
③ Safety information
3) Selected risk control measures are recorded in the Risk Management
Report (701-3).
4) If it is determined that the risk reduction required during the risk control
alternative analysis is impossible, a ‘risk/benefit analysis’ of the residual
risk is performed.
5.8.3 Implementation of Risk Control Measure(s)
1) The development team leader implements the risk control measures
selected from the alternative analysis.
2) After verifying the risk control measures, record them in the risk
management file.
3) After verifying the effectiveness of risk control measures, the results are
recorded in the risk management file.
5.8.4 Residual Risk Evaluation
1) After risk control measures are implemented, residual risk is assessed
based on criteria defined in the risk management plan. The results of this
assessment are recorded in the Risk Management Report (701-3).
2) If the residual risk is determined to be unacceptable based on these
criteria, additional risk control measures are applied.
3) Determine what residual risks are to be disclosed for the residual risks
judged to be acceptable and what information needs to be included in the
supporting documents to disclose these residual risks.
5.8.5 Risk/Benefit Analysis
1) If the residual risk is determined to be unacceptable based on the criteria
established in the risk management plan and further risk control is no
longer feasible, relevant information and literature are collected and

5.9 Risk Acceptability Criteria
5.9.1 Two Areas of Risk
1) Risk management does not define acceptable risks; it determines
acceptable risks.
2) Risks are classified into the following two areas
① Widely acceptable area (GREEN AREA)
– The risks are relatively low compared to the benefits of using
medical devices.
② Unacceptable areas (RED AREA)
– Some risks that cannot be reduced are always considered
unacceptable. One or more risk control measures must be
implemented to reduce the risk to an acceptable level.
5.9.2 Risk Tolerance Criteria
1) The stages of likelihood and severity should be set to at least three levels,
and the risk tolerance standard should be determined based on the
acceptable risk level for each product.

5.10 Overall Residual Risk Acceptability Assessment
5.10.1 After all risk control measures have been implemented and verified, the
quality manager uses the criteria defined in the risk management plan to
determine whether the overall residual risk posed by the medical device is
acceptable.
5.10.2 If the overall residual risk is determined to be unacceptable based on the
criteria established in the risk management plan, the quality manager
collects and reviews data to determine whether the medical device's benefits
for the intended use outweigh the overall residual risk. If this evidence
demonstrates that the medical benefits outweigh the overall residual risk,
the overall residual risk can be considered acceptable. Otherwise, the overall
residual risk remains unacceptable.
5.10.3 For the overall residual risk determined to be acceptable, the quality
manager determines what information to include in the supporting
documents to communicate that residual risk.
5.10.4 The results of the overall residual risk assessment are recorded in the risk
management report (701-3).
5.11 Risk Management Report
5.11.1 Prior to commercial sale of a medical device, the quality manager reviews
the risk management process.
5.11.2 The review should ensure at least the following.
1) The risk management plan is properly implemented.
2) The overall residual risk is acceptable.
3) Appropriate methods are in place to obtain relevant production and post-
production information, and the results of these reviews are recorded in
the risk management report and included in the risk management file.
4) The risk management plan is approved by appropriately authorized and
qualified personnel.
5.12 Production and Post-Production Information
5.12.1 During the production and post-production stages, the quality manager
establishes, documents, and maintains a system for collecting and reviewing
information about the medical device or similar devices under production.
5.12.2 When establishing a system to collect and review information about medical
devices, the quality manager shall consider the following.

1) Information generated during the production process and production
process monitoring.
2) Information generated by users.
3) Information generated by those responsible for the installation, use, and

4) Information generated by the supply chain.
5) Information made available to the general public.
6) Information related to generally accepted state-of-the-art technology.

5.12.3 When determining whether the collected information is related to safety,
refer to the following.
1) Are there any previously unrecognized risk factors or hazardous
situations?
2) Are the assessed risks arising from the hazardous situation no longer
acceptable?
5.12.4 If any of the above situations apply, evaluate the impact on previously
implemented risk management activities and provide feedback as input to
the risk management process.
5.12.5 If the residual risk, or its acceptability, is likely to change, reassess the
impact of previously implemented risk control measures.
