1. Overview
1.1 Document Overview
This document aims to provide cybersecurity lifecycle risk analysis and threat modeling for Digital
X-ray Imaging System (HnX-R1).
1.2 Medical Device Overview

Intended use The G/GT series Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector is indicated for
digital imaging solution designed for providing general
radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and
children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic
diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnosis and
treatment planning on a real time basis for physicians and
radiologists.
The G/GT series wired/wireless digital flat panel X-ray detector consists of the detector, power
supply box, battery pack, battery charger, other accessories, and software (SDK and firmware) for
image acquisition and image processing, and does not include the X-ray generator or console
application software. The detectors are of wired/wireless type, and there are 8 types in the G series
(G1417CW, G1417CWP, G1417FW, G1417FWP, G1717CW, G1717CWP, G1717FW, G1717FWP)
and 2 types in the GT series (GT1717C, GT1717CP) as a wired type.
This device is used to capture images by receiving X-ray transmitted through a patient’s body. The
X-ray passing through a patient’s body is sent to detector and then converted into electrical signals.
These signals go through the process of amplification and digital data conversion in the signal
processing device before being transmitted to the host console application via Ethernet of WiFi
interface using the SDK. The console application, which handles DICOM formatting, image display,
and communication with the Picture Archiving & Communication System (PACS) server, is not
included in this device's configuration. (A cybersecurity-compliant console application must be
used)
The cybersecurity risk of the G/GT series detector have been addressed to assure that no new or
increased cybersecurity risks were introduced as a part of device risk analysis. These risks are
defined as sequence of events leading to a hazardous situation, and the control for their risks were
treated and implemented as proposed in the risk analysis (e.g., requirements, verification).

The wired and wireless G series detectors are functionally identical in terms of software and
firmware. The wired GT series detector is a derivative of the G series, with wireless features and
other auxiliary functions removed. Therefore, cybersecurity testing and related documentation were
primarily conducted and prepared based on the G series. For the GT series, WiFi-related threats and
controls are not applicable.

4. SW Supply Chain Security System Diagnosis and
SBOM Analysis Results Report
The above Software library (2. SBOM list and Support Lifecycle), which includes both the library and open source
used in the console application and the detector/firmware, was evaluated for commonly known vulnerabilities and
CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures) on a site that can be verified as open source using the three tools
below, and it was confirmed that "there are no known vulnerabilities." This analysis was conducted through the
Korea Internet & Security Agency (KISA) Software Supply Chain Department.
Diagnostic tool: 3 types of diagnostic tools
Sparrow SCA, BlackDuck, Mend
Diagnostic result: No weakest content in the diagnostic result
4.1 Diagnostic Results
-. Number of detected components: Total number of components discovered
-. Number of vulnerable components: Number of components with 1 or more CVE vulnerabilities discovered
-. Number of security vulnerabilities: Total number of security vulnerabilities discovered (based on CVE)
-. Ratio of vulnerable components: (Number of vulnerable components/Number of detected components)*100(%)
