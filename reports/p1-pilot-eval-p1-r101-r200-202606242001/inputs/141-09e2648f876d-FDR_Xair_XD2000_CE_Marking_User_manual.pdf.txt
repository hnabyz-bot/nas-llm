Introduction
The XD2000 is a “battery-type portable X-ray system” which provides image data of the human
body for diagnosis by utilizing the scintillation effect, photographic effect and ionization effect of
X-rays permeated through the human body.
The XD2000 needs to be used in combination with the holding device XD2000 ST, XD2000 ST-M or
XD2000 ST-S. Other devices cannot be used in combination with the XD2000.
The X-ray generator is provided with a scale, AC adapter and shoulder strap.
This Operation Manual includes descriptions of matters necessary when using the battery-type
portable X-ray system XD2000 (hereafter referred to as the “XD2000”), such as the equipment
overview, operation procedures and precautions to observe, as well as daily inspections and
maintenance.
Since the XD2000 is designed to be portable, it can be carried into a patient’s home. In that case,
the XD2000 is generally used for X-ray radiography at the patient’s bedside (home health care).
The XD2000 can also be carried into a long-term care health facility for radiography at the patient’s
bedside or in the imaging room.
The XD2000 can be used for irradiating X-ray onto the human body at the time of X-ray radiography.
Intended purpose
The XD2000 is a battery-type portable X-ray system intended for use by qualified physicians and
technologists in generating radiographic images of human anatomy. Subjects to be examined are
adult patients. (Pediatric patients and animals are not included.)
The XD2000 is intended to be used in home health care and similar conditions. It is not intended for
mammography, fluoroscopy, tomography, and angiography applications.
Do not use the XD2000 for any purposes other than diagnosis. This equipment is not intended for
therapeutic use and it has no relation to therapeutic effects.

Installation may only be conducted by authorized service personnel.

1.3 Safety
Before using the XD2000, read this section thoroughly to ensure that you use the product properly.
Electric Shock Warnings and Cautions
WARNING
● The power supply to this equipment is AC100 to 240V.
To avoid electric shocks, users should always take the following precautions:
• Do not open any covers of the equipment.
• Place the equipment in a location where it will not be exposed to water.
• Do not make X-ray exposures while charging the battery.
● Do not disassemble or remodel each device. Otherwise, fire or electric shock may result.
Keep away from the parts inside the product, which may cause electric shock. If you touch
them accidentally, death or severe injury may result.
● Do not use the equipment in a location where metal particles could come into the equipment.
This may cause an electric shock.
● Do not use a power strip or extension cable for charging the battery. Otherwise, fire or
electric shock may occur due to the electrical load exceeding the allowable limit.
● Observe the following precautions when using the cables.
• Do not touch the plug with wet hands. Otherwise, electric shock may result, causing death
or severe injury.
• Hold the plug or connector when removing the cable. Pulling the cable or carrying by
holding it may damage the cable, causing fire or electric shock.
• Do not damage or remodel the cable. Do not place a heavy object on the cable. Do not step
on, pull, forcibly bend, or bundle the cable. Otherwise, fire or electric shock may result.
● Do not turn on the system or charge the battery with dew condensation on each device.
Otherwise, fire or electric shock may result.
● Do not hit or drop each device or subject it to severe shock. Otherwise, the device may be
damaged. If the damaged device is used, fire or electric shock may result.
● Make sure to use the optional parts recommended by us. Failure to use the optional parts
recommended by us may result in damage to the equipment and/or electric shock and injury.
CAUTIONS
● Keep each device away from patient’s body fluids, chemicals, water, etc. Otherwise, it may
become damaged, causing fire or electric shock. If necessary, protect the X-ray detector by
covering it with a disposable bag.
● If any fluid splashes onto the surface of the equipment, wipe it off immediately. If any
fluid soaks into the internal electronic components, it may cause equipment failure or
malfunction.
● Avoid rapid changes in temperature and humidity even within the specified range.
Otherwise, dew condensation may occur, causing equipment failure or rust and corrosion
inside the equipment.
Explosion Warnings
WARNING
● Because each device is not explosion-proof, do not use combustible and explosive gases
near each device.
● Flammable gasses may stay in the room after disinfection. If you turn the system on just
after disinfection, ensure that the room is well ventilated before powering on the system.

Warnings for Abnormalities
WARNING
● If any of the following occurs, immediately turn off the power of each unit, unplug the power
cable from the outlet, and then contact our official dealer or FUJIFILM Representative.
• When smoke, strange odor, or abnormal sound is present.
• When a foreign object (such as a metal object) or liquid enters the product.
• When the equipment is dropped or hit and is damaged.
Radiation safety instructions
WARNING
● Only qualified technologists are allowed to make X-ray exposures.
● Exposure should be made in an environment where the technologist can visually and aurally
determine the patient’ s condition.
● Technologists must wear an individual radiation exposure dosimeter to monitor exposure
doses they receive.
● Wrong operations of the X-ray generator could expose a technologist, patient or person in
the vicinity of the X-ray generator to unnecessary X-ray exposure. Technologists must keep
a distance of at least 2m from the focal spot and X-ray beam when making an exposure.
In addition, before making an exposure, ensure that persons other than the patient keep a
distance of at least 2m from the focal spot and X-ray beam.
● Confirm the radiation field size by using the light field lamp of the collimator. If the radiation
field size is unnecessarily large, the patient may be subject to unnecessary X-ray exposure.
● Reduce the radiation field size to the minimum necessary size for the exposure. If X-rays are
unavoidably radiated to areas outside of the subject region, take protective measures such
as wearing a protective apron with a lead equivalence of at least 0.25mm.
● When the patient needs assistance during an exposure, the assistant should take protective
measures such as wearing a protective apron with a lead equivalence of at least 0.25mm.
● Use the longest possible focal spot to skin distance to minimize the exposure dose.
● When making X-ray exposures, if the patient needs to be supported frequently, the patient
must be supported by a person registered as a radiation worker with an individual radiation
exposure dosimeter worn.
● Take extreme care when making a long exposure or repeated exposures. The patient’s health
may be compromised.
Warnings on Carrying This Product
WARNING
● Be sure to observe the following precautions when carrying this product. Collision or
dropping of the X-ray generator may cause not only damage to the X-ray generator, but also
serious accidents.
• Before carrying this product, attach the shoulder strap to the X-ray generator, ensure that
the strap is attached securely, and then wear the shoulder strap across your body.
• Before carrying this product, bundle the hand switch cable with the attached band so that
the cable does not hang down. Otherwise, the cable may get caught on a door knob, etc.,
and the equipment may fall onto the floor, resulting in equipment damage.
• Before carrying this product, ensure that the switch is attached and fixed securely to the
main unit of the X-ray generator
• Carry this product while paying attention to the difference in level on the floor.
• When carrying the holding device, follow the instructions provided in the operation manual
of the holding device.
• Turn off the power before carrying the X-ray generator.
• Before carrying, place each lock mechanism in a lock state and put the parts in cases.

X-Ray Generator Battery Instructions
CAUTIONS
If the following precautions are not observed properly, it may cause injury or unrecoverable
damage to the battery or X-ray generator.
● Battery requires regular checkup and replacement. Battery capacity begins to wane after a
period of time.
● The X-ray generator uses the lithium-ion battery. Use the X-ray generator under the
conditions of use specified by FUJIFILM Corporation. If the battery is used in a place where
it is exposed to high temperature, the battery may emit smoke, ignite, explode or leak fluid.
● It is recommended to replace the battery once every 2 years.
● When the battery for the X-ray generator needs to be replaced, consult our official dealer or
FUJIFILM Representative. In addition, dispose of the battery as stipulated by local low or any
regulations that apply.
● Before using or carrying the X-ray generator, be sure to check that the remaining battery
capacity is sufficient. If the remaining battery capacity is insufficient, charge the battery by
connecting the power cable. If no charging status is displayed even after connecting the AC
adapter, consult our official dealer or FUJIFILM Representative.
● Do not modify or replace the battery. In addition, if the battery needs to be repaired, consult
our official dealer or FUJIFILM Representative.
● Do not use the battery not recommended by FUJIFILM Corporation since it may cause
damage to the equipment.
● When the X-ray generator is not used, keep the battery in a state in which the two or more
battery indicators are lit.
● Charge the X-ray generator at least once every 3 months. Also charge the X-ray generator
when all examinations using the X-ray generator are finished at the end of the day.
● Do not use the X-ray generator on a regular basis under conditions where the remaining
battery capacity is insufficient (only one battery indicator is lit).

● Do not leave the battery for the X-ray generator unused for a long period of time since the

1.5.6 X-ray Irradiation
• Explain to the patient about the necessity of X-ray radiography and radiation protection, and use

5.1 Daily User Inspection and Maintenance
During maintenance and inspection, strictly observe precautions contained in “Chapter 1 For Safe
Operation” in this manual for you to use the XD2000 under best conditions.
5.1.1 Daily Inspection
Be sure to inspect the following items before use and ensure that the equipment is not faulty. If any
abnormality is found in the equipment, do not use the equipment.

Cleaning Before Use
To clean the outer surfaces, use a cleaning cloth tightly wrung out of commercially available ethanol.
CAUTIONS
● Be sure to turn off the power before performing cleaning and disinfection of the X-ray
generator. If the power is left on, it may result in unintended operations.
● Do not use an excessive amount of ethanol, as doing so may allow the liquid to enter from

the gap on the outer surfaces, resulting in the damage to the equipment, or cause the labels
to come off. Make sure not to let ethanol get inside the equipment.
● Do not use a solvent such as thinner or benzine, as it corrodes the outer surfaces.
● Use dehydrated ethanol or ethanol diluted with purified water.
● When cleaning or disinfecting the operation panel, observe the following precautions.
• Do not rub or hit the operation panel with something hard since it can easily be scratched.
• If any dust adheres to the operation panel, wipe it off with a soft cloth moistened with water.
5.1.2 Term of Validity/Period for Use (Durability) of XD2000
The term of validity (durability) is 6 years from first use of the X-ray generator, provided that the
precautions for use are followed and that the X-ray generator undergoes regular maintenance and
inspection. “Based on our company’s criteria”
5.1.3 Aging
All portable x-ray units require aging to protect the x-ray tube from an instant surge of current that
may damage your device and to insure the longevity and continued safe operation of your system.
Aging of the x-ray tube is required before use.
The following aging procedure is recommended.
Wear appropriate protective equipment and install the equipment where safety against radiation is
secured.
To perform aging of the filament of the X-ray tube, make X-ray exposures at intervals of 15 seconds.
(1) 1st time: 3 consecutive exposures at 50kV/1.0mAs
(2) 2nd time: 3 consecutive exposures at 60kV/1.2mAs
(3) 3rd time: 3 consecutive radiations at 90kV/2.0mAs
CAUTIONS
● While making an X-ray exposure, mechanical sound is heard from the equipment.
When the exposure is completed, the equipment is placed in wait mode, and the equipment
is not operational until the “Wait” lamp turns off.
● To prevent human body from being subjected to exposure, radiate X-ray toward the floor
(when aging is performed at the lowest floor) or toward an X-ray shielding material.
● Take care not to radiate X-ray toward the radiography devices during aging.

Appendix Z Precautions for Exposure
Z.1 Information on Radiation
Z.1.1 Radiation Protection
The operator has an obligation to follow the local laws and regulations to minimize radiation
exposure to the patients and medical professionals. For persons other than patients, a minimum
distance from the equipment is specified in each area.
Z.1.2 Deterministic Effects
In diagnostic radiology, the organ and tissue of interest are skin and crystalline lens, and the
threshold value of the exposure dose ranges from 1 Gy to 3 Gy.
When an exposure is made under the conditions which maximize the entrance surface dose (90kV,
2.5mAs, SID = 100cm), the reference value of the entrance surface dose is 2.5mGy.
During an examination, if 40 or more consecutive exposures are made under the conditions which
maximize the entrance surface dose, deterministic effects due to radiation may occur in patients.
When using the equipment, the operator should control the exposure dose appropriately,
considering deterministic effects.
