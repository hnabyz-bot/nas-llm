Introduction

This document specifies a process for a manufacturer, H&abyz Co., Ltd. to analyze, specify, design, verify and validate usability, as it relates to safety of a medical device, Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector (Model name: B1417MCW/B1717MCW). This usability engineering process assesses and mitigates risks caused by usability problems associated with correct use and use errors, i.e., normal use. it can be used to identify but does not assess or mitigate risks associated with abnormal use.

The B1417MCW/B1717MCW (Digital Flat Panel X-Ray Detector) is indicated as a digital imaging solution designed for providing the general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adults and children. It is intended to replace film-based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnoses for physicians and radiologists. Not to be used for mammography.

Operating principle

The X-ray output from the X-ray generator passes through the object to be photographed and is then converted into a digital signal through the detector to be output to the image signal. X-rays weaken in dense tissue such as bones, and soft tissue uses the principle of easy passage to adjust the brightness of the image to express the signal.

The indirect type detector consists of a Scintillator for converting X-rays into visible light and a TFT panel for converting them into data. A TFT array consists of covered with a thin layer of silicon that is amorphous or disordered, and silicon is engraved with millions of transistors arranged in higher order like a grid of graph paper. Each of these thin-film transistors (TFTs) is attached to a light-absorbing photodiode that makes up individual pixels, and visible light entering the photodiode is converted by intensity into a digital signal and output to the screen in a readable digital image.

Perform Summative Evaluation of the usability of the user interface

The summative evaluation report contains the following information:

For each hazard-related scenario, a test record be established with:

The hazard-related scenario and alternative evaluated,

The goal of the evaluation,

The user-profile participating to the evaluation,

The environment of evaluation,

The duration of the evaluation,

The IFU provided to the user or no IFU,

The results of the evaluation,

A rationale for setting the result to (i) OK, (ii) not OK, (iii) more information needed, given the goal of the evaluation.

The summative evaluation report ends with a discussion on the overall compliance of the results obtained for all tests. The result of the discussion is one of the three following states:

Compliant:

All tests are OK,

All risks related to usability are acceptable,

No additional data required,

Partially compliant:

At least one test is not OK,

No unacceptable risk is remaining but at least one risk is tolerable,

Additional data shall be collected by PMS and/or PMCF,

Not compliant:

At least one test is not OK,

At least one unacceptable risk is remaining,

Additional studies or design changes are required.

The summative evaluation report is recorded in Usability Validation Report (Doc. No. A-TCF-10-107).
