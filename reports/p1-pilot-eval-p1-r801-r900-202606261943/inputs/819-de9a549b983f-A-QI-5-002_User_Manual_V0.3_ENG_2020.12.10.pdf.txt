2. Product Introduction and Specification
2.1 Product Features
The HAD1717MC and HAD1717MG are wired digital flat panel detectors that have been designed
for faster, more streamlined approach to digital radiography systems. The HAD1717MC and
HAD1717MG detector utilize a combination of propriety TFT glass and scintillators(CsI and GDX), and
those and electronics are housed in one package. The detectors support an auto-trigger signal
sensing technology that allows the detectors to be used without generator integration.
The flat panel sensors of the HAD1717MC and HAD1717MG are fabricated using thin film
technology based on amorphous silicon technology. Electronically, the sensors are much like
conventional photodiode arrays. Each pixel in the array consists of a light‐sensing photodiode and a
switching Thin Film Transistor (TFT) in the same electronic circuit. Amorphous silicon photodiodes are
sensitive to visible light, with a response curve roughly comparable to human vision. The sensitivity of
amorphous silicon photodiodes peaks in green wavelengths, well matched to scintillators such as CsI
or Gd2O2S:Tb. The response has the excellent linearity of a charge‐integrating-biased photodiode.
Intended Use of Device
HAD1717MC and HAD1717MG are indicated for digital imaging solution designed for general
radiographic system for human anatomy. It is intended to replace film or screen based radiographic
systems in all general purpose diagnostic procedures. Not to be used for mammography.
2.1.1 Intended patient population
Radiographic imaging of human body parts such as head, Chest, spinal, abdominal, particular, hand,
foot and other internal organs.
a) Age: Available all people (Not to be used for infant or pregnant women)
b) Weight: not relevant
c) Height: not relevant
d) Nationality: multiple
e) Patient group/state: No specification
d) Applications parts: Part of body
f) Disease: Radiographic imaging of human body parts such as head, Chest, spinal, abdominal,
particular, hand, foot and other internal organs.

5. Maintenance
5.1 Cleaning
 Clean the detector with IPA (Isopropyl-alcohol) when it is contaminated.
 Before cleaning the detector, turn off the power and separate the battery.
 Wear waterproof gloves to protect your hands from direct contact with IPA or any other
liquid.
 Do not pour or spray IPA directly on the detector. Use fabric or soft cloth moistened with
IPA to clean.
 Avoid getting IPA or any other liquid into the detector.
 After cleaning, wait until the IPA is dried completely.
5.2 Inspection

the product regularly before use.
