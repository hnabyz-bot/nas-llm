HnX-P1, HnX-PB Document 1/72
Clinical Report
Model name: HnX-P1, HnX-PB
Test Condition
- Objective X-ray source : HnX-P1 made by H&abyz.
(The variant model HnX-PB has only changed the exterior design compared to the main model HnX-P1,
and the interface, exterior material, internal structure, PCB, software, etc. are all the same. Therefore, the
clinical study was conducted through the main model (HnX-P1))
- By these presents output data for communications standard DICOM 3.0 is used to format at the data for
downloading and analysis.
- FDA Cleared Detector (K223930) : A1717MCW
- The test is conducted by installing the portable device on a stand.
- Method: for each of image, one radiologist evaluated the images according to the criteria that is prepared
by manufacturer. Check grade: S, A, B, C
- The number of radiologist : 1 (Dr. Sung Hyun Kim, Seoul National University Hospital)
- The number of cases of each type: just one case is compared respectively.
- Patient of each case: different (described in the report, Evaluator 30)
- Technique factors of each case: same or different (described in the report)
- The method of data analysis: for each image, we arranged the evaluation content and one radiologist
evaluated the image from the clinical point of view.
- Intel i5 8GB RAM, Storage 500GB
Indication for Use
The portable x-ray system may be used for diagnostic imaging of body extremities.
- Not for mammography use
- This device is not intended to replace a stationary radiographic system, which may be required
for full optimization of image quality and radiation exposure for different exam types.

* Rationale for Patient(Subject) Selection
X-ray does not differ in image quality or output characteristics by race, and the results may
vary depending on the individual physical characteristics of the clinical subject, such as body
thickness and BMI index. The selection of Patient (Subject) of this product includes people
with a standard body type (BMI 20-30) who satisfy the indication for use, and additionally,
the Indication for Use of the product generally specifies bariatric patients defined as BMI 30
or higher as the exclusion target, but considering the worst case, we selected subjects (BMI
30-40) in the range of up to BMI40, which corresponds to the 95th percentile of adult male
BMI, as the worst case and performed a clinical evaluation on thick areas (abdomen, chest,
skull).
Adult BMI example (CDC Standard)
Weight Range BMI Range BMI Category
56.2kg or less Less than 18.5 Underweight
56.3kg to 76.2kg 18.5 to <25 Healthy Weight
76.3 kg to 91.6kg 25 to 30 Overweight
91.7kg or more 30 or greater Obesity
91.8kg to 107.0kg 30 to <35 Class 1 Obesity
107.1kg to 122.4kg 35 to <40 Class 2 Obesity
122.5kg or more 40 or greater Class 3 Obesity
(Severe Obesity)
[Table. NCHS - Vital Health Statistics Series 3, No. 39 report]
* For Patients with a BMI of 30 or higher, it may be difficult to accurately evaluate thick areas
such as the pelvis and hips using Hnx-P1

The images acquired using the HnX-P1 and HnX-PB were analyzed and evaluated for
image quality.

bone, with well-defined bone structures that allow for easy distinction.

visualized, and the differentiation between bones and joints was distinctly observed.
