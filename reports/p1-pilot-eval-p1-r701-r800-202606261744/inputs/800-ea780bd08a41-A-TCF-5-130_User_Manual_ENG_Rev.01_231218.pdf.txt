2. Product Introduction and Specification
2.1 Product Features
The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are wired or wireless digital flat panel detectors that
have been designed for faster, more streamlined approach to digital radiography systems. The
detector consists of TFT and Scintillator (CsI) and an electronic device that converts data read
through it into digital signals. The detectors support an auto exposure detection technology
that allows the detectors to be used without generator integration.
The flat panel sensors of the A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are fabricated using thin film
technology based on amorphous silicon technology. Electronically, the sensors are much like
conventional photodiode arrays. Each pixel in the array consists of a light‐sensing photodiode
and a switching Thin Film Transistor (TFT) in the same electronic circuit. The amorphous silicon
photodiode is sensitive to visual light, and its response curve has almost the same

28
performance as human vision. The sensitivity of amorphous silicon photodiodes peaks in green
wavelengths, well matched to scintillators such as CsI. It also has excellent linearity according
to the exposure dose.
Intended purpose
The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW (Digital Flat Panel X-Ray Detector) is indicated as a
digital imaging solution designed for providing the general radiographic diagnosis of human
anatomy targeting both adults and children. It is intended to replace film-based radiographic
diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnoses for physicians and radiologists.
Not to be used for mammography.
Indications
It is intended to replace film-based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise
case diagnoses for physicians and radiologists.
Contraindications
Local regulations should be reviewed before using those who have limitations in X-rays due to
health issues. Make sure to check the conditions of X-ray irradiation in advance.
Not to be used for mammography and direct cardiac application.
Not to be used for infant and pregnant women.
Target patient group
Adult and children except pregnant women.

About 5 years.
Applied part and duration of application
Patient contacts the front of the detector in less than 10 minutes.

2.3 Performance Principle
The X-ray output from the X-ray generator passes through the object to be photographed and
is then converted into a digital signal through the detector to be output to the image signal.
X-rays weaken in dense tissue such as bones, and soft tissue uses the principle of easy passage
to adjust the brightness of the image to express the signal.
The indirect type detector consists of a Scintillator for converting X-rays into visible light and a
TFT panel for converting them into data. A TFT array consists of covered with a thin layer of
silicon that is amorphous or disordered, and silicon is engraved with millions of transistors
arranged in higher order like a grid of graph paper. Each of these thin-film transistors (TFTs) is
attached to a light-absorbing photodiode that makes up individual pixels, and visible light

34
entering the photodiode is converted by intensity into a digital signal and output to the screen
in a readable digital image.

⚫ Product Set-Up
1) Connection methods of each operation mode
➢ AED Mode (Auto Exposure Detection Mode, Not use switch box)
The user can acquire images without additional connection between the X-ray system,
which is already installed, and the detector. When X-rays are exposed by an exposure
switch of the installed X-ray system, the detector detects the X-rays that are absorbed
without any additional external exposure signals and convert them into digital signals.
Converted digital data is delivered to PC via AP (Access Point) or main cable, and PC
converts the delivered data into images to show it on the monitor.
※ Object, PC, X-ray System are not included in this component.

62
➢ Normal Mode (with Switch Box)
The user can acquire images by connecting Switch Box to X-ray system that is already
installed. Normal Mode is to send exposure signals to PC and X-ray System using
Switch Box and a separate exposure switch. By pressing the exposure switch connected
to Switch Box, the detector is activated and ‘Ready’ sign can be seen. When prepared,
the detector delivers exposure signals to X-ray System.
The detector converts absorbed x-ray to digital data, delivers it to PC via AP (Access
Point) or main cable, and PC converts the delivered digital data into images to show it
on the monitor.
※ Object, PC, X-ray System and Control panel are not included in this component.
• The detector operates in a wireless state when using battery power
alone, and in a wired state when using a data cable.
