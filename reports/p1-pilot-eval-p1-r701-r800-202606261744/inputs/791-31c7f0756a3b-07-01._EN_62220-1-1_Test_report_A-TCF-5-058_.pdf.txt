- Generally, the actual effects and extent of aliasing artifact in image is the integrated value
under MTF curve above the cut-off frequency (nyquist frequency). The ideal value of MTF at cut-
off frequency is zero. However, real measured pre-sampling MTF at cut-off frequency is over
zero.
The detector element determines the spatial resolution capability of the detector. Its pitch of
this system is 0.14 mm corresponding to a sampling cut-off frequency of 3.57 lp/mm. The MTF
value at this frequency is approximately close to zero. For average human observers, MTF values
around 0.1 are considered barely observable. Therefore, artifacts in the image due to aliasing will
be negligible.
In addition, no evidence of aliasing was observed in a test pattern image acquired with this
detector (shown below). If there is aliasing artifacts, near nyquist frequency, the white and black
spokes may reverse - ie, white bars become black and black become white. This occurs between
frequencies 1/(pixels width) and 2/(pixels width), and is one kind of aliasing artifact..

2.3.12. Provide a plot of the latent image decay characteristic as a function of time and temperature.
Also describe any impact on signal retention as a function of the number of erasures and/or
exposures the device has experienced.
When the X-ray exposure is ended, the image is read immediately by the TFT detector and hence
no image decay is possible. To reduce thermal difference or variation due to surrounding conditions
of the detector, and to maintain the offset at the same level with each image acquired, the system
have stable power supply system. There is therefore no significant change due to changes in
surrounding conditions.
2.3.13. Recovery Time for radiographic devices: Indicate the time after readout is complete (including

any preparation function) until the device is able to accept the next exposure.
- Readout of the detector after X-ray exposure requires at least 10 seconds at which time the
detector is ready for the next exposure. Image readout, transferring and pre-processing requires
under 5 seconds.
