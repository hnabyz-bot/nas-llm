General requirements

General description of the product

General details

The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are wired or wireless digital flat panel detectors that have been designed for faster, more streamlined approach to digital radiography systems. The detector consists of TFT and Scintillator (CsI) and an electronic device that converts data read through it into digital signals. The detectors support an auto exposure detection technology that allows the detectors to be used without generator integration.

The flat panel sensors of the A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW are fabricated using thin film technology based on amorphous silicon technology. Electronically, the sensors are much like conventional photodiode arrays. Each pixel in the array consists of a light‐sensing photodiode and a switching Thin Film Transistor (TFT) in the same electronic circuit. The amorphous silicon photodiode is sensitive to visual light, and its response curve has almost the same performance as human vision. The sensitivity of amorphous silicon photodiodes peaks in green wavelengths, well matched to scintillators such as CsI. It also has excellent linearity according to the exposure dose.

Intended Purpose

The A1717MCW/A1417MCW/F1417MCW is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnosis and treatment planning on a real time basis for physicians and radiologists. Not to be used for mammography.

Operating Principle

The X-ray output from the X-ray generator passes through the object to be photographed and is then converted into a digital signal through the detector to be output to the image signal. X-rays weaken in dense tissue such as bones, and soft tissue uses the principle of easy passage to adjust the brightness of the image to express the signal.

The indirect type detector consists of a Scintillator for converting X-rays into visible light and a TFT panel for converting them into data. A TFT array consists of covered with a thin layer of silicon that is amorphous or disordered, and silicon is engraved with millions of transistors arranged in higher order like a grid of graph paper. Each of these thin-film transistors (TFTs) is attached to a light-absorbing photodiode that makes up individual pixels, and visible light entering the photodiode is converted by intensity into a digital signal and output to the screen in a readable digital image.

Benefit-risk analysis

General

ISO 14971:2019 allows the manufacturer to perform a benefit-risk analysis for those risks that are not judged acceptable using the criteria established in the risk management plan and for which further risk control is not practicable. The benefit-risk analysis is used to determine if the residual risk is outweighed by the expected benefits of the intended use of the medical device.

Benefit estimation

Benefit can be estimated from knowledge of several factors such as:

The performance expected during clinical use

The clinical outcome expected from that performance.

Benefits resulting from the use of similar medical devices.

Factors relevant to the risks and benefits of other diagnosis or treatment options.

The type of expected benefits for the patient or other people.

The magnitude of the expected benefits.

The probability that the patient will experience the expected benefits

The duration of the expected effects.

Criteria for benefit-risk analysis

In accordance with ISO 14971:2019, the benefit-risk analysis for the individual risk and the overall benefit-risk analysis should be implemented regardless of whether the risk is in the Green Zone or Red Zone after implementing risk control measures and evaluating residual risk(s).

Benefit-risk comparison

If contrast is necessary for risk benefit analysis, we will proceed with the following considerations.

Characterization of the disease or condition of the intended patients;

The uncertainty of data. Initially, a literature search for the hazards and the medical device being considered can provide insight into the balance between benefit and risk;

Production and post-production information for similar medical devices that are already available on the market;

The generally acknowledged state of the art;

A comparison of the benefits of the medical device under development with the benefits of similar medical devices available on the market;

A comparison of the residual risks of the medical device under development with the residual risks of similar medical devices available on the market.
