1.0 OVERVIEW
This risk management plan identifies all potential hazards and reviews, calculates, and plans all activities to be
performed in order to systematically manage risks by monitoring the effectiveness of control and calculating the
probability and severity of identified hazards in medical imaging equipment (HAD1717MG, HAD1717MC)
manufactured by H &abyz Co., Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as 'we').
2.0 Scope of Risk Management Plan
2.1 Related Standard and Requirements
Legal requirements
 Council Directive 93/42/EEC as amended by 2007/47/EC of 5 September 2007 concerning medical
devices.
 Medical Device Act, Enforcement Regulations of the Medical Device Act
 Standards for Manufacture and Quality Management of Medical Device
Standard requirements
 EN ISO 14971: 2012 (Medical Device Risk Management)
 IEC 60601-1[2006/A1:2013] Medical electrical equipment Part 1: General requirements
 IEC 60601-1-2[2015] Medical electrical equipment Part 1: General requirements

3.0 Terms and Definitions
Adapted from EN ISO 14971:2012 Cl. 2. Terms and definitions
3.1 Accompanying document: Document accompanying a medical device and containing information for
those accountable for the installation, use and maintenance of the medical device, the operator or the user,
particularly regarding safety
3.2 Harm: Physical injury or damage to the health of people, or damage to property or the environment
3.3 Hazard: Potential source of harm
3.4 Hazardous situation: Circumstance in which people, property, or the environment are exposed to one or
more hazard(s)
3.5 Intended use & Intended purpose: Use for which a product, process or service is intended according to the
specifications, instructions and information provided by the manufacturer
3.6 Life-cycle: All phases in the life of a medical device, from the initial conception to final decommissioning
and disposal
3.7 Manufacturers: natural or legal person with responsibility for the design, manufacture, packaging, or
labelling of a medical device, assembling a system, or adapting a medical device before it is placed on the
market or put into service, regardless of whether these operations are carried out by that person or on that
person's behalf by a third party
3.8 Medical device: Any instrument, apparatus, implement, machine, appliance, implant, in vitro reagent or
calibrator, software, material or other similar or related article, intended by the manufacturer to be used,
alone or in combination, for human beings for one or more of the specific purpose(s) of
- diagnosis, prevention, monitoring, treatment or alleviation of disease,
- diagnosis, monitoring, treatment, alleviation of or compensation for an injury,
- investigation, replacement, modification, or support of the anatomy or of a physiological process,
- supporting or sustaining life,
- control of conception,
- disinfection of medical devices,
- providing information for medical purposes by means of in vitro examination of specimens derived from the human
body, and which does not achieve its primary intended action in or on the human body by pharmacological,
immunological or metabolic means, but which may be assisted in its function by such means
- aids for disabled/handicapped people,
- devices for the treatment/diagnosis of diseases and injuries in animals,
- accessories for medical devices (see Note 3),
- disinfection substances,
- devices incorporating animal and human tissues which can meet the requirements of the above definition but are
subject to different controls.

3.9 Objective evidence: Data supporting the existence or verity of something
3.10 Post-production: Part of the life-cycle of the product after the design has been completed and the medical
device has been manufactured
3.11 Procedure: Specified way to carry out an activity or a process
3.12 Process: Set of interrelated or interacting activities which transforms inputs into outputs
3.13 Record: Document stating results achieved or providing evidence of activities performed
3.14 Residual risk: Risk remaining after risk control measures has been taken
3.15 Risk: Combination of the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of that harm
3.16 Risk analysis: Systematic use of available information to identify hazards and to estimate the risk
3.17 Risk assessment: Overall process comprising a risk analysis and a risk evaluation
3.18 Risk control: Process in which decisions are made and measures implemented by which risks are

3.19 Risk estimation: Process used to assign values to the probability of occurrence of harm and the severity of

acceptability of the risk

tasks of analyzing, evaluating, controlling and monitoring risk
3.22 Risk management file: Set of records and other documents that are produced by risk management
3.23 Safety: Freedom from unacceptable risk
3.24 Severity: Measure of the possible consequences of a hazard
3.25 Top management: Person or group of people who direct(s) and control(s) a manufacturer at the highest
level
3.26 Use error: Act or omission of an act that results in a different medical device response than intended by
the manufacturer or expected by the user
3.27 Verification: Confirmation, through the provision of objective evidence, that specified requirements have

4.0 Description of Device
4.1 Intended Use of Device
HAD1717MC and HAD1717MG are indicated for digital imaging solution designed for general radiographic
system for human anatomy. It is intended to replace film or screen based radiographic systems in all general
purpose diagnostic procedures. Not to be used for mammography. The device also includes software and
software that convert medical images to digital and store and transmit images using computerized radiographic
imaging (CR) and digital radiography (DR) devices.
4.2 Device Description
Product name Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector
Model name HAD1717MG, HAD1717MC
Class Class Ⅱa
Manufacturer H&abyz Co.,Ltd.
Country South Korea
4.2.1 Performance
1) Electrical rate : 100~240V / 50-60Hz / MAX.80W
2) Sensor Type : a-Si, TFT
3) Scintillator : HAD1717MG(Gd2O2S:Tb) / HAD1717MC(Csl:Tl)
4) Total Pixel Number/Area : 3072x3072 pixels / 430x430 mm
5) Effective Pixel Number/Area : 3052x3052 pixels / 427x427 mm
6) Pixel pitch : 140um
7) Limiting Resolution : Max 3.5lp/mm
8) Energy Range : 40~150kv
9) A/D Conversion : 14/16bits
10) Preview/Cycle time : About 2.0sec / About 3.0sec
11) Communication : 1Giga-bit Ethernet
12) Trigger Mode : Line(Manual) Trigger, Auto Trigger(Auto Exposure Detection)

5.0 Life Cycle Application of Risk Management
5.1 This management plan applies the entire life cycle of the product, which includes Design Plan, Design &
Development, Design Verification, Design Validation, Purchase, Production, Inspection, Packing & Shipping
and after sales service.
The figure below illustrates the flow of the cycle.
5.2 The activities required to be carried during each stage of the life cycle of the product are as follows;
① Design & Development Plan:
During Design & Development step, the state of reported customer complaints and after sales services
shall be examined to analyze risk factors. For the entire risk factors identified, the level of risks in normal
and abnormal operating conditions shall be evaluated. The company shall determine whether the
occurrence possibility of the identified risks has been reduced after evaluation and the result shall be
used as design input factors. The entire process of Design & Development Plan step shall be carried
out and reviewed the head of R&D, the quality manager and development personnel and, if necessary,
medical specialists shall participate in the process.
② Design & Development:
The design and development inputs include the intended use, functional and efficient safety, and control
requirements. The previous lists of the expected risk factors can be derived by checking the related
criteria and surveillance database, and the product inspection report. The risk factors have to be available

③ Design Verification:
Objective evidences of the fact that the introduction and effectiveness verification of the risk management

production, measuring and monitoring equipment, considering the risks related to process. The
production environment and workers shall be controlled by the risk management methods, documented
and evaluated regularly. The validation of the production process is affected by the result of risk
management measures and carried out using such risk management methods as FMEA.
⑥ Packing & Shipping:
The company shall identify the risks that affect product’s quality during the packing and shipping steps and
the degree to which products are damaged during shipping or shipping workers are injured. The result
shall be reflected in the process to analyze the harm.
⑦ Post-Production Step:
The company shall collect information related to customer feedback including customer complaints and after
sales service requests after products are delivered to customers in accordance with relevant
procedures specified in the quality management system. In addition, the risk analysis shall be carried
out again using the information acquired after sales and the risk management report shall be reviewed
and revised, if necessary.
⑧ Monitoring:
The company shall monitor risk related matters during the entire steps of product life cycle and perform
statistical analyses using the information obtained from monitoring
