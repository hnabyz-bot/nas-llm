Introduction

This device (B1417MCW/B1717MCW) intended use is as follows:

This product is intended to obtain the information necessary for the diagnosis of the patient through the anatomical structure of human body obtained by a film or an image processing (workstation) process of examination using radiation exposure with cassette IP, CR or DR (Portable flat panel).

The B1417MCW/B1717MCW, Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector is indicated for digital imaging solution designed for providing general radiographic diagnosis of human anatomy targeting both adult and children. It is intended to replace film based radiographic diagnostic systems and provide more precise case diagnosis and treatment planning on a real time basis for physicians and radiologists. Not to be used for mammography.

Among the accessories of the Digital Flat Panel X-ray Detector can be selected between B1417MCW, and B1717MCW.

Performance and usage are all the same except for size, so there is no difference in 'Usability'

(A-TCF -10-159)

A usability test is intended to determine the extent an interface facilitates a user’s ability to complete routine tasks. Typically, the test is conducted with a group of potential users with portable equipment. Users are asked to complete a series of routine tasks. Sessions are recorded and analyzed to identify potential areas for improvement to the B1417MCW/B1717MCW.

The usability test objectives are:

To construct the benchmarks of user attributes

To identify potential design problems to be solve

To improve product design and production efficiency

To complete end-user satisfaction maximization

The project team conducted an onsite usability test. A total 7 participants were involved in a usability test to ensure stable results. The test administrator contacted to attendees by informing the test logistics and requesting their availability and participation. Participants responded with an appropriate date and time. Each individual session lasted approximately one hour.

The purpose of the test was to complete the primary operating function described in usability engineering report without the issue regarding to safety and effectiveness.

This document contains the participant feedback, satisfactions ratings, task completion rates, ease or difficulty of completion ratings, time on task, errors, and recommendations for improvements. A copy of the questionnaires is included in the Attachment.

Further Improvement

Based the usability verification & evaluation report, some problems are carried out during the usability testing, session of participants interview. Proper risk control measures are required to implement into the design to reduce the derived risks.

Conclusion

It has been shown that the usability engineering activities are performed to full the usability engineering process. Usability verification & evaluation results and improvement action demonstrated that residual risk regarding usability is minimized and acceptable. Since the usability verification & evaluation report demonstrates that the usability goal met the acceptance criteria set in the usability verification & evaluation plan, no evaluation of “benefit outweigh risk” is performed. The re-evaluation of usability will be performed once new use-error issues are raised.
